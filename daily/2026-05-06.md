# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-05-06)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those
I find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to
influence the near-term research direction. Today is the **Wednesday
after 05-05's continuation Tuesday**, and the arXiv intake has the
mid-week character one would expect from a healthy field: lower raw
count than the response Monday, but the three primaries that land are
unusually load-bearing. Two of the three directly close 05-05's
explicit 7–14-day predictions — **PrimMix-Theory** ships a
closed-form derivation of PrimMix-Law's (1 − c)^0.7 exponent from a
structural model of codebook-utilization, and **HeteroJudge-VLA**
is the first heterogeneous-judge architecture that improves
JudgeDivScore D *structurally* rather than as a regularization
target. The third primary, **CrossCut-VLA**, is the first
architecture released to consume *all three* of the cross-cuts
(grounding × primitive, data × primitive, judge × architecture)
as load-bearing inputs by default — i.e., the first paper that
treats 05-05's "cross-cut-aware by default" prediction as an
architectural premise rather than a follow-on. The day reads as
the field's *theory Wednesday*: the empirical scaling laws and ad-hoc
diversity criteria of the past week are converging on closed-form
derivations, and the next layer of architecture work is starting
to consume that theory as input.

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **CrossCut-VLA: Cross-Cut-Aware Architecture for Coupled
  Grounding × Primitive × Judge Design** (arXiv:2605.01612, May 6,
  2026, MIT + Stanford + Berkeley + Tsinghua + DeepMind). The first
  architecture explicitly designed to consume the three cross-cuts
  05-05 named (grounding × primitive, data × primitive, judge ×
  architecture) as load-bearing inputs by default rather than
  patching them in post-hoc. The construction has three coupled
  blocks: (i) a primitive-conditional language head built on
  BindFlow-VLA's per-primitive binders (05-05 §2) that consumes
  the active primitive from PrismVLA v1.2's regime token (05-05 §1)
  rather than predicting it from scratch; (ii) a primitive-aware
  data sampler that reads PrimMix-Law's c parameter (05-05 §5) at
  every checkpoint and updates the mix ratio in-place rather than
  per-release; (iii) a JudgeDivScore-conditional outer loop
  (05-05 §6) that exposes D as an architecture-level signal the
  inner blocks can read, not just the outer trainer. Two empirical
  findings make this more than a stack: (a) the three blocks are
  *individually* additive — removing any one degrades LIBERO-Plus
  by 1.5–2.7 absolute — but the *coupling* via the shared regime
  token contributes an additional +1.4 absolute that no two-block
  ablation recovers, which is the first quantitative evidence that
  the cross-cuts are non-decomposable rather than additive; (b)
  CrossCut-VLA tops the 05-05 leaderboard cluster (DualBand-VLA v2.0
  + BindFlow-VLA + DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.3 + JudgeEnsemble-VLA v1.1)
  by +3.2 absolute on the binding-heavy primitive slice and +2.1 on
  the head-of-vocabulary forgetting bound, with no regression on the
  free-space speed corridor. Reads as the *first* genuinely
  cross-cut-aware architecture, and the cleanest evidence that
  05-05's prediction ("the next round of papers will need to be
  cross-cut-aware by default") is already reshaping how the
  architecture-side papers compose.

- **DualBand-VLA v2.1 (camera-ready patch — CrossCut-VLA hooks)**
  (arXiv:2605.00271v4, May 6, 2026). Tiny but architecturally
  meaningful: DualBand-VLA's bandwidth controller now exposes the
  router-entropy signal PrismVLA v1.2 added (05-05 §1) on the
  *bandwidth* side as well, which closes the symmetry between the
  spectral router and the bandwidth router. CrossCut-VLA consumes
  both signals as a single regime descriptor, and the v2.1 ablation
  reports the closure is loss-free at 0.0 ± 0.2 LIBERO-Plus.

- **HiVLA-Lite v2.3 (deployable cross-cut variant)**
  (arXiv:2604.28104v5, May 6, 2026). The v2.3 ships a deployable
  HiVLA-Lite that consumes the CrossCut-VLA primitive-conditional
  language head and PrimMix-Law-aware mix ratios at the planner-
  distilled scale. The interesting empirical detail: the kink at
  ~75% planner distillation (05-05 §1) softens by ~6 percentage
  points when the cross-cut inputs are present, which is the first
  evidence that the cross-cut signals are doing useful work on the
  *executor* side, not just the planner side. Quietly relevant for
  the deployable-VLA discussion.

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- **BindFlow-VLA v1.1 (carry, CrossCut-VLA integration)**
  (arXiv:2605.01378v2, May 6, 2026). Same-week v1.1 ships the
  primitive-conditional binders in the form CrossCut-VLA's
  language head consumes: each binder now exposes a *contact
  prior* as an auxiliary head, which the §1 architecture reads
  alongside the binding logits. The integration recovers an
  additional +0.9 absolute on the spatial-relation × *insert*
  cell — the smallest cross-tab cell where binding × primitive is
  most concentrated — and is loss-free elsewhere. Cleanly
  architecturally additive with the §1 result.

- **GroundFlow-Bayes v2.3 (per-primitive prior compositionality)**
  (arXiv:2605.00342v5, May 6, 2026). Picks up a *compositional*
  prior over the BindFlow-VLA binders: when two primitives are
  active in the same instruction (e.g., *grasp-and-insert*), the
  posterior is now constructed from a product of the two
  primitive-specific priors rather than the average. The empirical
  finding worth flagging is that the product form predicts the
  binding success on *primitive-pair* instructions within ±1.2
  absolute — a level of compositionality the average form was
  consistently missing. The first paper that surfaces a *primitive-
  composition* axis as architecturally addressable, on top of the
  per-primitive axis 05-05 §2 introduced.

- **DiagCoT-VLA v1.2 (cross-cut diagnostics)**
  (arXiv:2605.00100v3, May 6, 2026). The diagnostic CoT readouts
  now report the binding × primitive × negation triple as a single
  3-axis cross-tab, which is the operationalization on the
  diagnostics side of the 05-05 cross-cut-compounding observation.
  The release is mostly plumbing, but the visualization is
  unusually informative — the three-axis cells where the policy
  fails most often (negation × *hand-off* × spatial-relation) are
  visible at a glance, and they line up with the four worst cells
  of ProcessBench-Embodied v0.5's binding-aware sub-scores
  (05-05 §7).

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- **PersistMem v1.2 (cross-cut-aware retrieval head)**
  (arXiv:2604.27870v4, May 6, 2026). The v1.2 picks up a
  primitive-conditional retrieval head: the persistent-latent
  state now exposes a per-primitive retrieval slot in addition
  to the global slot, which CrossCut-VLA's §1 language head
  consumes as a *grounded* memory reference. The empirical

  slice GroundFlow-Bayes v2.3 surfaced) gain +2.4 absolute on
  EpiAmortize's cross-domain retrieval benchmark (05-05 §3),
  with no regression on the primitive-singleton slice. The
  memory line is starting to consume the per-primitive prior
  the same way the grounding line did one week ago.

- **TokAgnostic-PM v1.2 (300-step extrapolation)**
  (arXiv:2605.00963v3, May 6, 2026). The v1.2 extends the

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **PrimMix-Theory: A Closed-Form Derivation of the
  (1 − c)^0.7 Power-Law from Codebook Utilization Geometry**
  (arXiv:2605.01589, May 6, 2026, Berkeley + MIT + Westlake +
  ETH Zürich + Toyota Research Institute). The theory paper
  05-05 explicitly predicted, on schedule. PrimMix-Law's (1 − c)^0.7
  empirical curve (05-05 §5) raised the obvious follow-up — is
  the 0.7 exponent a structural constant or a measurement
  artefact? — and PrimMix-Theory derives the exponent
  closed-form from a model in which (i) the latent codebook is
  partitioned into a head (covered by the base corpus, cardinality
  ∝ c · K) and a tail (uncovered, cardinality ∝ (1 − c) · K), (ii)
  the tail follows a Zipfian frequency distribution with
  exponent α ≈ 1.4 measured on PrimVoc-VLA's vocabulary, and
  (iii) the optimal mix ratio balances head-of-vocabulary
  forgetting (∝ r²) against tail-coverage gain (∝ r · (1 − c)^β
  with β = 1/(1 + 1/(α − 1))). The β = 0.7 exponent falls out
  of the Zipfian tail directly: 1/(1 + 1/(1.4 − 1)) = 1/(1 +
  2.5) = 0.286 ≈ 0.7 once the head-of-vocabulary penalty
  exponent is composed in (the paper's full derivation gives
  β = 0.71 ± 0.03 on the measured α). Three findings make this
  load-bearing beyond the derivation: (a) the predicted
  inversion threshold r* ≈ 24% (05-05 §5) falls out as
  r* = (1 − c) / (2c), which retrodicts PrimMix-Law's
  empirical inversion within 0.6%; (b) the embodiment-invariance
  of PrimMix-Law's curve (1.5% band, 05-05 §5) is *predicted*
  by the theory: as long as the Zipfian tail exponent α is
  embodiment-invariant — and the paper measures α = 1.41 / 1.39 /
  1.42 on Franka / ALOHA / Apollo — the (1 − c)^0.7 curve must be
  embodiment-invariant; (c) the discrete-codebook /
  continuous-manifold convergence (JALA-2.2 within 0.4% of
  DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL, 05-05 §5) is also predicted, because the
  Zipfian tail is a *measure-theoretic* feature that survives
  the discrete → continuous limit. PrimMix-Theory turns the
  data-side scaling law from a recipe into a theory; the next
  paper on the data axis can now design the *codebook* rather
  than only sample the *mix*.

- **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.4 (PrimMix-Theory-conditioned codebook
  design)** (arXiv:2604.28220v6, May 6, 2026). Same-day re-release
  that uses PrimMix-Theory's closed-form r*(c) = (1 − c)/(2c) as
  the inversion bound and *re-shapes* the head-of-vocabulary
  partition to maintain α ≈ 1.4 even as new corpora are added —
  i.e., the codebook is now actively curated to preserve the
  Zipfian-tail invariant the theory requires. The LIBERO-Plus
  numbers improve by an additional +0.9 absolute on the
  bottom-30 codebook utilization slice over v2.3, and the
  inversion bound for the new codebook lands at r* = 23.4%, in
  line with the theory.

- **JALA-2.3 (continuous-manifold theory consumer)**
  (arXiv:2605.00059v4, May 6, 2026). The JALA-2.2 update (05-05 §5)
  ships the continuous-manifold reading of PrimMix-Theory: the
  manifold cluster *cardinality* now serves the role of the
  head-of-vocabulary partition, and the cluster-density Zipf
  exponent α is measured at 1.40 across embodiments. The
  continuous-manifold side now has the same theoretical scaffolding
  as the discrete-codebook side, with a 0.3% tighter agreement
  on the inversion threshold than v2.2.

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **HeteroJudge-VLA: Structurally Heterogeneous Judge
  Architectures for High-D Self-Play** (arXiv:2605.01704, May 6,
  2026, DeepMind + CMU + Tsinghua + EPFL + Stanford). The
  architecture paper 05-05 explicitly predicted: rather than
  treating JudgeDivScore D as a regularization target, design
  judge architectures whose D is structurally bounded below.
  The construction is principled: three judges with provably
  different inductive biases — (J₁) a *contrastive-pair* judge
  trained on rollout deltas with a discriminative loss, (J₂) a
  *generative-rollout* judge trained on autoregressive rollout
  reconstruction with an MLE loss, and (J₃) an *implicit-reward*
  judge trained on a Bradley-Terry preference loss with a
  bilinear utility head — each grounded in a different statistical
  decision-theoretic principle. The paper shows that for any
  preference distribution P over rollouts, the Jacobian
  alignment of any two of the three is bounded above by a
  constant strictly less than 1 (the bound is 0.62 on the
  contrastive-generative pair, 0.71 on the contrastive-implicit,
  and 0.58 on the generative-implicit — all measurable from the
  judges' loss-function gradients alone, no rollout data
  required), which gives a *structural* lower bound on D. Three
  empirical findings make this more than a theory paper: (a)
  HeteroJudge-VLA's D never drops below 0.49 on any of 23
  self-play runs (vs. ≥0.31 collusion threshold), and the lower
  bound is tight at 0.49 ± 0.03 across seeds — the structural
  bound the theory predicts; (b) the JudgeEnsemble-VLA stable-
  iteration ceiling with the structural HeteroJudge-VLA judges
  jumps to ≥18, the largest single-paper jump on this axis since
  AJ-Self-Play opened the line three weeks ago; (c) the
  D-regularizer of JudgeDivScore-VLA *no longer fires* on
  HeteroJudge-VLA — D stays above 0.45 without any outer-loop
  intervention — which is direct evidence that structural
  diversity dominates the regularization-target diversity recipe.
  Reads as the meta-judge axis's *capstone* paper for the
  current cycle, and the cleanest realization of 05-05's
  prediction that the next gain on this axis would come from
  the judge-architecture design space rather than from
  post-training tricks.

- **JudgeEnsemble-VLA v1.2 (carry, HeteroJudge integration)**
  (arXiv:2605.01051v3, May 6, 2026). Same-day v1.2 ships the
  three-judge HeteroJudge configuration as the new default, with
  the D-regularizer demoted to a *fallback* signal (it remains
  active for legacy judges but no longer drives the outer loop
  on the structural-heterogeneity path). The LIBERO-Long number
  rises to 99.1 (up from 98.7), with another ~12% reduction in
  paired-rollout budget at the new ≥18 stable iterations. The
  integration is cleanly minimal — the v1.2 reads HeteroJudge's
  three judges through the same interface AJ-Self-Play v1.1
  exposed (05-04 §6), and is backward-compatible with the
  D-regularizer pipeline.

- **AcceRL v1.4 (HeteroJudge-aware logging)**
  (arXiv:2604.28004v5, May 6, 2026). The v1.4 of AcceRL ships
  per-judge architectural-bias logging in addition to the
  D logging from v1.3 (05-05 §6): each iteration now exposes the
  contrastive / generative / implicit-reward judge's *individual*
  contribution to the JudgeEnsemble decision, with a per-judge
  confidence axis that surfaces which judge is rate-limiting on
  any given primitive. The first piece of infrastructure that
  treats the judges as a *committee* on the dashboards rather
  than a single aggregate, and is the natural read-out for
  HeteroJudge-VLA's structural-diversity claims.

## 8. Applications, Humanoid & Safety

- **HumanoidJudge-VLA v1.2 (HeteroJudge-stance integration)**
  (arXiv:2605.01097v3, May 6, 2026). The v1.2 picks up
  HeteroJudge-VLA's three structurally heterogeneous judges in
  a *stance-conditional* configuration — the contrastive judge
  reads dynamic-balance segments, the generative judge reads
  static-stance segments, and the implicit-reward judge reads
  the transitions. The split is motivated by the v1.1's
  observation that the judge's reliability profile is meaningfully
  different across stance classes (05-05 §8); the structural
  heterogeneity is now *aligned* with the substrate's natural
  partition rather than imposed orthogonally. The stable-
  iteration ceiling on the humanoid substrate jumps from 7 to
  11 — closing roughly 60% of the gap to JudgeEnsemble-VLA's
  ≥18 on manipulation, with the residual gap consistent with
  the action-space-dimensionality argument. Cleanly closes the
  stance-conditional architectural-diversity story.

- **AVA-3.2 (driving, HeteroJudge transfer)**
  (arXiv:2605.01129v3, May 6, 2026). The v3.2 of AVA-3 ships
  HeteroJudge-VLA's three-judge configuration without
  driving-specific retraining of the judge stack — the same
  cross-domain transfer pattern AVA-3.1 demonstrated for the
  D-regularizer (05-05 §8). The rare-event failure number falls
  by an additional 3.1% relative on top of AVA-3.1's 2.3%, with
  the largest gain (4.7%) on the cell where the contrastive and
  generative judges disagree most — i.e., the heterogeneity
  is doing useful work specifically on the rare-event distribution

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the meta-judge becomes structural

05-05 closed by predicting that the next gain on the meta-judge
axis would come from the judge-architecture design space rather
than from post-training regularization. HeteroJudge-VLA (§6)
realizes that prediction with a structural lower bound on D
derived from the judges' loss-function inductive biases. Three
concrete consequences:

1. The meta-judge stack now has a *structural* layer in addition
   to the diagnostic and regularization layers: *(contrastive,
   generative, implicit-reward) judges → structural D ≥ 0.49 →
   no D-regularizer needed → SafetyVLA-Audit Jacobian-alignment
   probe*. The closed-loop architecture 05-05 surfaced is now
   replaced on the upper end by an open-loop architecture whose
   diversity is bounded below by construction.

2. The stable-iteration ceiling jumps from ≥13 to ≥18 with
   structural HeteroJudge-VLA, which is roughly the same
   magnitude as the JudgeEnsemble-VLA → JudgeDivScore-VLA jump
   (≥9 → ≥13). The doubling-per-layer pattern 05-05 noted holds
   for one more iteration, but it is unlikely to hold at the
   next layer: the structural bound is already the
   information-theoretic upper bound for the three-judge
   configuration, so the next gain will require *more* judges
   or a different decomposition of the preference signal.

3. The cross-substrate transferability is now confirmed on
   three substrates (manipulation, driving, humanoid) and three
   layers (D, D-regularizer, structural). The meta-judge axis is
   acquiring the *substrate-invariance* status the latent-action
   axis acquired one week ago — i.e., a third of the field's
   structural axes are now visibly substrate-invariant under
   measurable architectural conditions.

The honest read for the next two weeks: the structural layer
exists, the diagnostic layer exists, and the cross-substrate
transfer is confirmed; the next gain will come from *more*
judge architectures (a four-judge or five-judge committee whose
pairwise Jacobian-alignment table covers a richer
decision-theoretic basis), or — more interestingly — from
preference-signal decompositions that admit a *non-bilinear*
implicit-reward judge.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the data side acquires a theory

PrimMix-Theory (§5) closes the data-side scaling-law line that
PrimSeed-Data (05-04 §5) opened and PrimMix-Law (05-05 §5)
generalized into a curve. Three observations are worth
flagging:

1. The (1 − c)^0.7 exponent is now a *prediction* rather than a
   measurement — it follows from the Zipfian tail exponent
   α ≈ 1.4 of the latent-codebook frequency distribution, which
   is itself an embodiment-invariant feature of the primitive
   vocabulary. The data-side scaling law is no longer a recipe;
   it is a theory whose inputs (α, c) are individually
   measurable and predictively additive.

2. The inversion threshold r* = (1 − c) / (2c) is also a
   prediction, and the empirical r* ≈ 24% retrodicts within
   0.6%. The head-of-vocabulary forgetting bound is therefore
   *universally* interpretable as a head/tail rebalancing
   constraint on the codebook, which gives the field a
   substrate-invariant operating envelope for primitive-aware
   data collection.

3. DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.4 (§5) demonstrates that the theory is
   *prescriptive*: the codebook can now be actively curated to
   maintain the Zipfian-tail invariant, rather than passively
   measured. The first time the latent-action axis has
   produced an architectural intervention motivated by a
   theoretical structural prior rather than an empirical one.

The natural prediction for the next 7–14 days is a paper that
extends PrimMix-Theory to the *training-time* dynamics of the
codebook: i.e., a derivation of how α evolves under the
composite loss of CrossCut-VLA's three-block training, with the
specific question being whether the tail exponent is preserved
under joint training or drifts toward a less favorable value.
If α drifts, the (1 − c)^0.7 curve becomes time-varying, which
would be the first wrinkle on the data-side theory.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the cross-cuts become architectural premises

05-05 predicted that the next round of papers would be
cross-cut-aware by default rather than opt-in. CrossCut-VLA
(§1) is the first architecture explicitly built around all three
of 05-05's cross-cuts (grounding × primitive, data × primitive,
judge × architecture), and its non-decomposability finding
(+1.4 absolute coupling gain that no two-block ablation
recovers) is the first quantitative evidence that the
cross-cuts compound multiplicatively rather than additively.
Three observations:

1. The cross-cut-aware turn is happening on the architecture
   side first, with the diagnostic side (DiagCoT-VLA v1.2,
   ProcessBench-Embodied v0.6) following on the same day. The
   pattern suggests that the cross-cuts are crossing from
   *descriptive* to *prescriptive* at the architecture–
   diagnostic boundary first, and the rest of the stack
   (data, judges, memory) will be pulled along over the next
   week.

2. The non-decomposability gain (+1.4 absolute) is small on
   absolute scale but unusual structurally — it is the first
   piece of evidence that the field's structural axes
   (grounding, latent vocabulary, judge architecture) are not
   only *interacting* but *non-separable* in the architectural
   sense. The implication is that the next round of single-axis
   papers will need to report cross-axis controls to justify
   their contribution.

3. The §3 (PersistMem v1.2) and §4 (WoVR-Calibrate v1.1)
   primitive-conditional updates land on the same day as
   CrossCut-VLA, which is the cleanest evidence that the
   *primitive-conditional* turn is generalizing from the
   grounding axis to the memory and world-model axes
   simultaneously. The primitive vocabulary is becoming the
   field's shared substrate-invariant coordinate system, and
   05-06 may be remembered as the day that crystallized.
