# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-05-03)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those I
find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to influence
the near-term research direction. Today's edition closes the
**May 1–3, 2026** window: a Sunday's arXiv intake is, as always, light
on net-new primaries, but the entries below cohere into a single
narrative that 05-02 explicitly predicted — the *self-improving judge
loop* lands one cycle early as **AJ-Self-Play**, the world-model and
memory branches publish their first joint paper (**PersistMem**) that
makes their convergence on the *amortization* view formal, and the
latent-action line gets its psycholinguistic mapping in **PrimVoc-VLA**.
Combined with a small batch of camera-ready / v2 updates that close
out April-cycle threads, the May 1–3 weekend reads as the cleanest
three-day arc the field has produced this year: 05-01 settled the
post-training stack's *cells*, 05-02 added the *judge*, and 05-03
closes the *loop*.

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **DualBand-VLA: Bandwidth-Conditioned Action Decoders for
  Generalist Manipulation** (arXiv:2605.00482, May 3, 2026, Stanford +
  CMU + NVIDIA). Direct architectural follow-up to *PrismVLA*
  (05-02 §1) that drops the hand-designed two-band split and replaces
  it with a learned per-segment *bandwidth controller* — a small
  classifier on the planner-side codebook that selects, at each
  segment boundary, the integration bandwidth its action decoder
  should target. The empirical headline is that the learned
  controller recovers PrismVLA's hand-tuned 5 Hz / 50 Hz split on
  contact-rich subsets *and* discovers a third regime — a low-jerk
  ~15 Hz mode for free-space approach with payload — that PrismVLA's
  two-band design could not express. Reads as the natural
  *meta-frequency* generalization of PrismVLA in the same way that
  AdaFlow-VLA was the meta-step generalization of one-shot flow
  matching: PrismVLA is to DualBand what fixed-step is to adaptive.
  +1.7 LIBERO-Plus over PrismVLA, +0.6 over AdaFlow ⊕ PrismVLA
  combined, with no wall-clock penalty.

- **MoT-π v1.1 (camera-ready)** (arXiv:2604.27340v3, May 3, 2026).
  The v1.1 carries the routing-stability ablation reviewers asked
  for: across 200 evaluation episodes, the mixture-of-tokenizers
  router switches an average of 3.2 times per trajectory, but the
  switches cluster *exactly* at the contact / no-contact boundaries
  rather than being distributed uniformly. Quietly important
  evidence that the routing signal is the *same* signal PrismVLA
  and DualBand-VLA condition on — the field is converging on
  contact-vs-no-contact as the single most useful per-segment
  control variable in the VLA stack.

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- **GroundFlow-Bayes (carry update)** (arXiv:2605.00342v2, May 3,
  2026). The v2 patch on yesterday's *GroundFlow-Audit* lands a
  Bayesian-VLA (04-19) replication of the failure-mode breakdown.
  The new numbers — 44% referent ambiguity, 31% spatial relation,
  18% attribute, 7% negation — are within 3 points of the
  GroundFlow-Audit result on a different benchmark and a different
  policy, which tightens the *Robust Skills, Brittle Grounding*
  (04-27 §2) taxonomy from "the right shape" to "the right
  numbers". The language-vs-skill axis now has its first
  cross-policy, cross-benchmark replication.

- **PointFlow-VLA: Coarse-to-Fine Referent Resolution via
  Pointed Tokens** (arXiv:2605.00518, May 3, 2026, Tsinghua + BAAI).
  Net-new entry that pushes Point-VLA's (04-19) pointing-as-grounding
  recipe a level finer: instead of one pointed token per referent,
  PointFlow emits a *coarse* point (object-level) followed by a
  *fine* point (part-level) inside the same trajectory, with a
  contrastive loss that ties the fine point to the manipulation
  contact site. Lifts the spatial-relation slice of GroundFlow-Audit
  by 9.1 absolute and the attribute slice by 4.4, which is exactly
  the breakdown one would predict if part-level grounding were the
  binding constraint. The cleanest piece of progress on the
  referent-grounding axis since PDF (04-26).

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- ★ **PersistMem: A Joint Latent State for Persistent World Models
  and Episodic Policy Memory** (arXiv:2605.00604, May 3, 2026,
  Westlake + CUHK + Tsinghua). The paper 05-02's cross-cutting
  observation predicted: *PersistDream* (05-01 §4) and *MemFlow-VLA*
  (05-01 §3) are unified as two readouts of a single *persistent
  latent state*. The construction is clean: at episode boundary, the
  shared latent absorbs (i) the world model's scene drift via a
  3D-consistency probe loss and (ii) the policy's task memory via
  EpiAmortize-style amortization, with a routing head selecting
  which readout each downstream consumer (dream rollout vs.
  retrieval) sees. Critically, the loss tying the two readouts is a
  *mutual-information* objective, not a hard alignment — the
  policy memory and the world memory are encouraged to share
  *factors* but not to collapse onto the same vector. The empirical
  headline: in joint training on 1,200 hours of manipulation data,
  PersistMem matches PersistDream on the new 200-step scene-drift
  eval and matches MemFlow-VLA on long-horizon retrieval, *and* the
  shared latent shows positive transfer in both directions
  (+2.4 retrieval, +1.8 scene-drift). The memory and world-model
  branches are now demonstrably the same factoring at two
  granularities, exactly as the 05-02 reading speculated.

- **EpiAmortize-Driving (carry, sharpened)** (arXiv:2605.00271v2,
  May 3, 2026). The wrapper paper from 05-02 §3 picks up an
  autonomous-driving evaluation track and folds in the AVA-2
  (05-02 §8) numbers as a baseline: with the rolling hazard buffer
  amortized, EpiAmortize-Driving cuts the rare-event miss rate by
  another 14% relative on top of AVA-2's 22%. The interesting
  number is the *adapter size*: 1.7M parameters per per-region
  adapter, fully shared across drivers in the same metropolitan
  region, which makes the deployment cost negligible. The
  cross-domain transfer narrative is now numerically credible at
  both manipulation and driving scales.

- **MAP-VLA-Long v1.2 (carry update)** (arXiv:2511.09516v4, May 3,
  2026). The v1.2 patch adds a memory-bank-size sweep over
  vla-eval-2.0's exposed hook (05-02 §7): MAP-VLA-Long degrades by
  6 points over a 4× bank growth, vs. EpiAmortize's 2 points and
  the un-amortized baseline's 18 points. The graceful-degradation
  metric now has three data points and a clear ordering — and
  MAP-VLA's prompt-side gain stacks additively with the
  amortization-side gain, as 05-02 already noted.

## 4. World-Model-Based VLA

- **WoVR-3: Composable In-World Verifiers for Long-Horizon
  Imagination** (arXiv:2605.00547, May 3, 2026, DeepMind +
  ETH Zürich). Direct successor to *WoVR-2* (05-01 §6) that drops
  the monolithic verifier in favor of a small set of *composable*
  per-axis verifiers (contact, kinematic feasibility,
  goal-progress, instruction adherence) that can be composed at
  inference time per task. Reports two findings that matter: (i) the
  contact verifier alone explains 58% of WoVR-2's gain on the
  contact-rich subset, with the other three axes contributing the
  remainder roughly equally; (ii) when ActionJudge (05-02 §6) is
  plugged in *as a fifth verifier*, the train-test gap closes a
  further 11% on top of the integration-test number from 05-02.
  The WM-side and judge-side verifiers continue to look
  complementary.

- **PersistDream-Eval v1.1 (humanoid extension)**
  (arXiv:2605.00187v2, May 3, 2026). The companion benchmark from
  05-02 §4 picks up a humanoid split derived from the
  ProcessBench-Embodied humanoid track. The 200-step scene-drift
  numbers transfer cleanly: PersistDream remains within probe
  tolerance through ~190 steps on humanoid (vs. ~210 on tabletop),
  while DreamZero and Veo-Act lose 3D consistency by step ~70 (vs.
  ~80 on tabletop). Useful evidence that the persistent-latent-state
  recipe is not tabletop-specific.

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **PrimVoc-VLA: A Psycholinguistic Mapping for Recovered Latent
  Action Primitives** (arXiv:2605.00633, May 3, 2026, Berkeley +
  MIT + Google DeepMind). The paper 05-02's latent-action
  cross-cutting observation predicted: an empirical alignment
  between the DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL codebook (05-02 §5), the JALA-2
  manifold clusters (05-01 §5), and a hand-curated
  psycholinguistic primitive set (grasp / push / pull / pour /
  insert / hand-off / track / align / pivot / tap). The construction
  is careful: PrimVoc-VLA does not *assume* the alignment, it
  *measures* it by training a linear probe from each codebook entry
  / manifold cluster to the human primitive labels and reporting
  the matched-pairs accuracy. The headline number is 78.4% top-1
  alignment between DiscreteFlow's top-30 entries and the
  10-primitive set, with 71.2% for JALA-2, and a Procrustes-style
  alignment between the two latent spaces of 0.83 cosine. Not
  every entry maps — *pivot* and *tap* are under-represented in
  both — but the alignment is far above chance and far above
  random-codebook baselines. This is the strongest piece of
  evidence yet that the *robot phonology* hypothesis is real:
  latent action primitives are a property of manipulation data,
  the recovered vocabulary is roughly architecture-invariant, and
  the missing primitives identify *which behaviors the field's
  data is under-representing*. Most consequential paper of the day
  for anyone tracking action representation.

- **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.1 (camera-ready, primitive-vocabulary
  patch)** (arXiv:2604.28220v3, May 3, 2026). Adds a small but
  important reanalysis: the codebook's *bottom*-30 entries (rather
  than top-30) cluster on rare and embodiment-specific behaviors
  (compliance-mode releases, two-finger pinch grasps, lateral
  scoops). When combined with PrimVoc-VLA's mapping, the picture
  is that the codebook learns a head-and-tail vocabulary — a small
  shared core that aligns with psycholinguistic primitives and a
  long tail of embodiment-specific specializations. This is the
  factoring the cross-embodiment transfer literature has been
  looking for an empirical anchor on.

  (05-01 §5), PELA-Robot (05-02 §5), UniT (04-24), JALA (04-22),

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **AJ-Self-Play: Closing the Self-Improving Judge Loop with
  In-World Preference Data** (arXiv:2605.00712, May 3, 2026,
  DeepMind + ETH Zürich + Stanford + Tsinghua). The paper 05-02
  explicitly predicted, arriving exactly one cycle later. The
  recipe is clean: (1) WoVR-3 (§4 above) is used as the imagination
  substrate; (2) the policy under training samples *paired*
  trajectory rollouts that differ only in a small action
  perturbation; (3) ActionJudge (05-02 §6) scores the pairs, with
  its calibrated abstention head dropping uncertain pairs;
  (4) the surviving high-confidence pairs are used to fine-tune a
  *new* ActionJudge, which is then folded back into the loop.
  Two findings make this paper non-obvious despite having been
  predicted: (i) the loop is stable for at least 5 outer iterations
  before judge-policy collusion symptoms appear, and the authors
  trace this to ActionJudge's preference loss being on
  *action-conditioned* outcomes rather than terminal-frame
  preference (the 05-02 architectural choice that mattered
  yesterday matters more today); (ii) the abstention head is the
  binding safety mechanism — disabling it collapses the loop into
  a degenerate equilibrium within 2 iterations. Plugged into the
  AlignRL-VLA + AcceRL + VLAJS + ActionJudge stack, AJ-Self-Play
  lifts LIBERO-Plus by +1.4 and the 47-task household eval by
  +3.7 absolute over 05-02's already-saturating numbers, while
  cutting the human preference budget by ~92%. This is the
  *fifth cell* of the post-training stack — a *self-improving
  judge* — and the recipe is now end-to-end human-label-light.

- **AcceRL v1.2 (camera-ready, AJ-Self-Play hooks)**
  (arXiv:2604.28004v3, May 3, 2026). The v1.2 lands the substrate
  changes AJ-Self-Play needed: paired-rollout sampling at
  WoVR-3-verifier granularity, abstention-aware preference logging,
  and a backup queue for the outer ActionJudge fine-tuning loop.
  Mostly an integration release, but the abstention-aware logging
  is genuinely new infrastructure — preference datasets that carry
  the judge's confidence are a notably better training signal than
  binary pairs.

- *(Carried primaries: ActionJudge (05-02 §6), AlignRL-VLA
  (05-01 §6), WoVR-2 (05-01 §6), VLAJS (04-26), AcceRL (04-24),
  SimpleVLA-RL @ ICLR 2026 (LIBERO-Long 97.6), FMER (04-22),

## 8. Applications, Humanoid & Safety

- ★ **AVA-2.5 + Self-Play Hazards** (arXiv:2605.00768, May 3, 2026,
  Tsinghua + DiDi + Wayve). Direct follow-up to *AVA-2*
  (05-02 §8) that ports the AJ-Self-Play loop (§6 above) to the
  driving domain: paired rollouts of corner-case scenarios are
  scored by an ActionJudge variant trained on driving preferences,
  and the surviving high-confidence pairs are used to fine-tune the
  judge in-domain. The interesting empirical finding is that the
  driving judge needs *fewer* outer iterations than the
  manipulation judge (3 vs. 5 before degenerate equilibrium
  symptoms appear), which the authors trace to driving's lower
  action-space dimensionality narrowing the perturbation envelope.
  +9.1% relative reduction in rare-event failure on top of AVA-2's
  combined gain. The cross-domain transfer of the *self-improving
  judge* recipe is now empirically established.

- **HTD-Touch v2 (camera-ready)** (arXiv:2604.27870v3, May 3, 2026).
  WholeBodyVLA-Touch's tactile-prediction head from 05-02 §8 picks
  up an explicit grounding tie to the PrimVoc-VLA primitive set
  (§5): the tactile head's contact-onset signal is shown to align
  exactly with the *grasp* and *insert* primitives 81% of the time,
  and the misalignments are concentrated on the *pivot* and *tap*
  primitives that PrimVoc-VLA flagged as under-represented in the
  data. Useful evidence that the tactile-prediction objective is
  doing exactly the work the latent-vocabulary line predicted —
  filling in the primitives the visual-only data could not learn.

- **SafetyVLA-Audit v3 (humanoid + driving extension)**
  (arXiv:2604.26110v3, May 3, 2026). Adds a sixth risk axis —
  *judge-policy collusion under self-play* — that the AJ-Self-Play
  recipe (§6) made necessary. The probe checks whether a self-play
  fine-tuned judge has drifted toward rewarding behaviors that look
  good but fail under sparse-success ground truth, and the
  diagnostic threshold is set conservatively: any drift > 4% on
  the held-out sparse-success set triggers a warning. Two of the
  three self-play VLAs in the public leaderboard pass; the third
  (a non-AJ-Self-Play self-improving variant) fails, which is
  exactly the kind of differential signal the safety side of the
  stack should be producing.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the post-training loop closes

05-01 settled the post-training stack's three cells (reward,
substrate, regularizer); 05-02 added the fourth (judge); 05-03
closes the loop with the fifth — a *self-improving judge*:

> *VLAJS jump-start → AcceRL substrate → WoVR-3 imagination →
> ActionJudge preference oracle → AJ-Self-Play outer loop.*

The five together are now the cleanest end-to-end recipe the field
has, and crucially the recipe is *human-label-light* end-to-end:
~92% of ActionJudge's preference budget can be replaced by self-play
data without a stability cost, provided three architectural
ingredients hold: (a) action-conditioned preference loss (so the
judge cannot be fooled by terminal-frame realism), (b) calibrated
abstention (so degenerate equilibria are filtered before they
propagate), and (c) composable in-world verifiers (so the
imagination substrate exposes per-axis disagreement signals to the
preference labeler). The next visible gap is on the *evaluation*
side, not the training side — the LBM Eval v3.0 latent-vocabulary
track (§7) is the first sign the field is starting to evaluate
*what* the policy learned, not just *whether* it succeeds.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — memory and world-model branches formally unify

PersistMem (§3) is the paper 05-02 implicitly predicted: the
*amortization* view that MemFlow-VLA (05-01) introduced for episodic
policy memory and the *persistent latent state* view that
PersistDream (05-01) introduced for scene memory are made formally
the same in PersistMem — two readouts of a single shared latent,
tied by mutual information rather than hard alignment. Three
consequences worth tracking:

1. The factoring is *layer-agnostic*: amortization at the policy
   layer and amortization at the WM layer are the same
   architectural operation at different granularities.
2. Cross-domain transfer is *expected*, not surprising: the AVA-2
   (05-02) and EpiAmortize-Driving (§3) results stop being
   coincidences and start being predictions of the unified factoring.
3. The next research push is *shared-latent training stability*:
   the mutual-information loss is the right objective in principle,
   but PersistMem reports it is finicky in practice (sensitive to
   the world-model's video-tokenizer choice, in particular). This
   is the obvious target for the next 30 days of work on this
   thread.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — robot phonology is testable

PrimVoc-VLA (§5) gives the latent-action vocabulary line its first
*falsifiable* claim: a 10-primitive psycholinguistic set aligns
with the recovered DiscreteFlow / JALA-2 vocabularies at 78.4% /
71.2% top-1, and the misalignments are not random — they cluster
on *pivot* and *tap*, which both DiscreteFlow's bottom-30 codebook
analysis and HTD-Touch's tactile-misalignment data independently
identify as data-poor primitives. This is the cleanest evidence yet
that the recovered vocabulary is a property of the data and that
the missing primitives are *diagnosable*. The implication for data
collection is direct: the field's next data push should be
explicitly biased toward pivot, tap, and the other under-represented
primitives, not toward more unconditional teleop volume. LBM Eval
v3.0's primitive-alignment leaderboard track (§7) is the right
pressure to make this happen.
