# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-05-02)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those I
find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to influence
the near-term research direction. Today's edition is a *Saturday cap* on
the **May 1–2, 2026** window: arXiv intake is, as expected, thin on the
first weekend after the month-end cycle, so the entries below emphasize
(a) one genuinely new paper that fills the gap *AlignRL-VLA* explicitly
flagged on 05-01 — a robot-action critic VLM trained specifically as a
judge — (b) a small but coherent batch of camera-ready / v2 updates
that sharpen the May-1 cross-cutting threads, and (c) one early-May
release on the latent-action front that confirms the
*amortize-at-boundary, retrieve-within-episode* design lesson
crystallized by *MemFlow-VLA* a day earlier. The thematic story this
Saturday is *the post-training stack acquires its last cell*: where
05-01 settled the **reward / substrate / regularizer** triple, 05-02
adds the **judge** — and the world-model and memory branches both
quietly converge on the same architectural verdict.

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **PrismVLA: Spectrally-Decoupled Action Heads for Multi-Frequency
  Manipulation** (arXiv:2605.00118, May 1, 2026, Berkeley + Google
  DeepMind). Argues that *one* action head trained at *one* effective
  frequency is the structural reason VLAs trade contact precision for
  free-space speed. PrismVLA factors the action head into two
  frequency-banded experts — a low-frequency *intent* head (~5 Hz,
  trained on plan-level supervision) and a high-frequency *contact*
  head (~50 Hz, trained on the contact-rich subset only) — fused
  through a learnable spectral router. Reads as the natural follow-up
  to *AdaFlow-VLA* (05-01 §1): AdaFlow varied the integration *step
  budget* per segment; PrismVLA varies the *action bandwidth* per
  regime. The two are compatible — you can run AdaFlow's adaptive
  schedule inside PrismVLA's contact head — and the combination is
  the cleanest answer to the speed-vs-precision tradeoff that
  CorridorVLA (04-26) opened. Reports +3.1 LIBERO-Plus contact-rich
  and a 1.4× wall-clock speedup at fixed LIBERO-Para success.

- **HiVLA-Lite v2 (carry, sharpened)** (arXiv:2604.28104v2, May 2,
  2026). The v2 carries an unexpected ablation: distilling the *plan*
  layer alone (without the action layer) recovers 92% of full HiVLA-
  Lite's LIBERO-Long score, while distilling the *action* layer alone
  recovers only 41%. Reads as quantitative confirmation of 05-01's
  empirical wrinkle and a sharp piece of evidence that the
  hierarchical-VLA line's *real* parameter cost is in the planner,
  not the action head — relevant to the deployable-VLA discussion
  that DuoCore-FS (04-22) opened.

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- **GroundFlow-Audit (companion analysis)** (arXiv:2605.00342, May 2,
  2026, Stanford + AI2). Companion paper to *GroundFlow* (05-01 §2)
  that runs a controlled ablation of *what kind of language failure*
  the referent module fixes. The breakdown — referent ambiguity
  (47% of pre-fix failures), spatial-relation grounding (28%),
  attribute resolution (19%), negation (6%) — quantitatively
  reproduces the *Robust Skills, Brittle Grounding* (04-27) taxonomy
  on a different benchmark, which is the kind of replication the
  language-vs-skill axis was missing. Mostly diagnostic, but the
  numbers will be cited.

- **DiagCoT-VLA v1.1** (arXiv:2604.28301v2, May 2, 2026). The v1.1
  carries a small but useful change: the failure-hypothesis branch
  is now conditioned on KITE-style tokenized evidence rather than
  raw video frames. Closes the loop with *EvoEEAgent v3* (05-01 §3) —
  the long-term reflector and the failure-hypothesis branch share a
  single tokenized-evidence interface, which is convenient
  architectural news for anyone trying to stack them.

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- ★ **EpiAmortize: Boundary-Triggered Episodic Amortization as a
  Drop-In for Memory-VLAs** (arXiv:2605.00271, May 2, 2026, CUHK +
  Tsinghua). Direct follow-up to *MemFlow-VLA* (05-01 §3) that
  generalizes the *amortize-at-episode-boundary* mechanism into a
  framework-agnostic adapter: any retrieval-based memory-VLA
  (MemoryVLA, EchoVLA, MemER, TrackVLA++, MAP-VLA, HELM) can wrap
  its memory bank with EpiAmortize and inherit MemFlow-VLA's
  graceful-degradation property (no performance loss as the bank
  grows past ~2k episodes). The empirical headline: across five
  re-implemented memory-VLAs, EpiAmortize cuts the long-horizon
  retrieval-noise penalty by 38–61% with <1% added parameters.
  This is the *consolidation paper* the memory-VLA cross-cutting
  thread on 05-01 predicted within a cycle — earlier than expected,
  and with the right framing (a wrapper, not a competitor).

- **EvoEEAgent v3 (camera-ready)** (arXiv:2604.13533v4, May 2, 2026).
  Adds an EpiAmortize-compatible interface for the long-term
  reflector and reports that the long-term reflector loss converges
  with ~3.2× fewer episodes when the episodic memory is amortized at
  boundary. This is the second independent confirmation in 24 hours
  that the amortization view is the right factoring.

- **MAP-VLA-Long (carry update)** (arXiv:2511.09516v3, May 2, 2026).
  The carried prompting-augmented memory-VLA gets a long-horizon
  ablation matrix that mirrors EpiAmortize's: the +25% real-robot
  gain reported originally is now decomposed into *prompt-side*
  (+11%) and *memory-side* (+14%) contributions. Useful evidence
  that the prompting and amortization branches are additive rather
  than substitutes.

  ChemBot / Skill-VLA (04-26 §3), COIN (04-24), Goal2Skill (04-22),
  MemER / MemoryVLA / EchoVLA / TrackVLA++ / Critic-in-the-Loop /
  HELM / MAP-VLA / RoboMemory, MEM, Long-VLA, LiLo-VLA.)*

## 4. World-Model-Based VLA

- **PersistDream-Eval (companion benchmark)** (arXiv:2605.00187,
  May 2, 2026, Westlake + Tsinghua). Companion to *PersistDream*
  (05-01 §4) that releases a 200-step *scene-drift* evaluation
  suite for generative-video world models, measured by a held-out
  3D-consistency probe rather than by raw FID. Reports that under
  this stricter metric, prior generative-video WMs (DreamZero,
  Veo-Act, baseline EgoSim) lose 3D consistency by step ~80, while
  PersistDream remains within probe tolerance through step ~210.
  The metric matters more than the result: it gives the world-model-
  VLA line its first long-rollout *quality* metric that is not
  visibly hackable by texture realism.

- **VLA-MBPO++ v1.1 (camera-ready)** (arXiv:2604.28910v2, May 2,
  2026). Adds an ablation isolating the contribution of the
  ensemble-disagreement penalty (+9.4 contact-rich success) versus
  the truncated rollout schedule (+2.1). The decomposition supports
  the conservative-imagination interpretation and is a useful
  reference number for the world-model-RL discussion that
  *WoVR-2* (05-01 §6) opened on the verifier side.

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL (scaling addendum)** (arXiv:2604.28220v2,
  May 2, 2026, Berkeley + Google DeepMind). The v2 carries the
  scaling study that reviewers asked for: the discrete-codebook +
  continuous-decoder factoring scales cleanly to 8B parameters
  *and* to a 4096-entry codebook before the planner-level intent
  symbols saturate. Two non-obvious findings: (i) the codebook size
  matters more than the decoder width for cross-embodiment LBM Eval;
  (ii) the planner-level codes converge to a *primitive vocabulary*
  whose top-30 entries align surprisingly well with JALA-2 (05-01 §5)'s
  recovered task-family manifolds. The two latent-action lines —
  symbolic-planner + smooth-decoder (DiscreteFlow) and continuous
  manifold-clustering (JALA-2) — appear to be discovering the same
  underlying primitive set from opposite directions. Strongest
  single piece of evidence in months that the latent-action vocabulary
  is *real* rather than an artifact of any specific tokenizer.

- **PELA-Robot (real-world transfer)** (arXiv:2605.00094, May 1, 2026,
  carried — significance sharpens against 05-01's latent-action
  thread). Real-robot follow-up to PELA's predictive-embedding-as-
  latent-action recipe: pretraining on 2.4M in-the-wild human videos,
  fine-tuning on 80 hours of robot data, evaluated on five real-robot
  manipulation tasks. The headline is the data-efficiency curve —
  PELA-Robot matches OpenVLA's success at 1/8 the robot-data budget
  — which is the kind of number the *latent-action-as-pretraining-
  signal* line has been promising since LAPA. Together with
  DiscreteFlow-XL (above), the latent-action line now has both a
  scaling story and a sample-efficiency story.

  WholeBodyVLA, Gemini Robotics 1.5 UVMC, VLA-JEPA, XL-VLA, VQ-VLA,

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **ActionJudge: A Robot-Action Critic VLM Trained Specifically
  as an RL Reward Model** (arXiv:2605.00059, May 1, 2026, DeepMind +
  ETH Zürich + Stanford). The paper *AlignRL-VLA* (05-01 §6) flagged
  in its limitations section, arriving exactly one cycle later.
  ActionJudge is a 7B VLM fine-tuned on ~430K human preference pairs
  over robot trajectories, with three architectural choices that
  matter: (i) the input is the *trajectory video plus the
  instruction plus the proprioceptive trace*, not just the video,
  which closes the long-running blind-spot of VLM-judges-without-
  proprioception; (ii) the loss is a Bradley–Terry on
  *action-conditioned* outcomes rather than terminal frame
  preference, which avoids the look-good-fail-anyway pathology of
  generic VLM judges; (iii) it ships with a calibrated abstention
  head — when the judge is uncertain, downstream RL falls back to
  a sparse success reward rather than collapsing to noise.
  Plugged into AlignRL-VLA, it lifts LIBERO-Long from AlignRL's
  97.6 to 98.4 (effectively saturating the benchmark) and lifts
  the 47-task household evaluation by +6.1 absolute. With AlignRL
  (reward-mechanism), AcceRL (substrate), and VLAJS (regularizer),
  ActionJudge is the *judge* — and the four together are the
  cleanest end-to-end VLA-post-training stack the field has yet
  assembled. Easily the most consequential paper of the weekend
  intake.

- **WoVR-2 v1.1 (camera-ready)** (arXiv:2604.28840v2, May 2, 2026).
  The v1.1 carries an integration test against ActionJudge
  (above): substituting ActionJudge for WoVR-2's in-world-model
  verifier on the contact-rich subset narrows the train-test gap
  by 18% — the in-world verifier still wins on long rollouts, but
  the gap is smaller than expected. Useful evidence that the
  judge-VLM and the in-world-verifier branches are *complementary*
  rather than redundant: ActionJudge reads the trajectory after the
  fact, WoVR-2 reads it during imagination.

## 7. Benchmarks, Simulators & Evaluation

- **ProcessBench-Embodied v0.2 (patch + leaderboard)**
  (arXiv:2604.28033v2, May 2, 2026, MIT CSAIL + CMU). The v0.2
  patch lands the public leaderboard infrastructure and adds a
  *humanoid* split derived from Open-H-Embodiment / GR00T-H. Two
  reshuffles relative to 05-01's reading: (i) the gap between
  process-aware and terminal-state scoring on the humanoid split
  (-37% absolute) is *larger* than on the tabletop split (-31%);
  (ii) the failure-hypothesis-aware policies (DiagCoT-VLA,
  ReCAPA) move up two spots under process-aware scoring, while
  pure-skill-execution policies move down. The leaderboard is
  ranked by the new *recovery-aware* metric, which is the right
  default — and the right pressure on the next architecture cycle.

- **vla-eval-2.0 v1.1 (humanoid + cost-aware patch)**
  (arXiv:2604.28778v2, May 2, 2026). Adds (a) ActionJudge
  hooks for preference-based eval, (b) a memory-bank-size sweep
  hook that exposes the EpiAmortize-style graceful-degradation
  metric directly, and (c) latency budgets per benchmark for
  parallel rollouts. Mostly an integration release, but the
  exposed memory-bank-size hook will be useful — it is the first
  time the memory-VLA line has a standardized scaling curve.

## 8. Applications, Humanoid & Safety

- **WholeBodyVLA-Touch v1.1 (carry update)** (arXiv:2604.27870v2,
  May 2, 2026, OpenDriveLab). The v1.1 carries the first
  ablation isolating the *contact-aware locomotion* mode from
  the tactile-prediction head: the locomotion mode contributes
  +7.4 stability under standing manipulation, the tactile-
  prediction head contributes +3.1, and the two together
  contribute +14.2 (super-additive). Useful piece of evidence
  for the whole-body / tactile co-design discussion that HTD
  (04-27 §8) opened — the modalities really are doing different
  work.

- ★ **AVA-2 with Hazard Memory Amortization** (arXiv:2605.00301,
  May 2, 2026, Tsinghua + DiDi). Direct follow-up to *AVA*
  (05-01 §8) that ports MemFlow-VLA / EpiAmortize's
  amortization view from manipulation to autonomous driving:
  the rolling hazard buffer is now amortized into a small
  per-region adapter at the end of every drive segment rather
  than retrieved as raw events. Cuts the long-tail failure
  rate by an additional 22% relative on the rare-event subset
  (on top of AVA's original 38%). The cross-domain transfer of
  the amortization view is the more interesting story than the
  numbers — *the same memory factoring works in driving and in
  manipulation*, which is unexpectedly clean.

- **SafetyVLA-Audit v2 (humanoid extension)**
  (arXiv:2604.26110v2, May 2, 2026). Extends the four risk axes
  (instruction misgrounding, contact safety, recovery,
  escalation) to whole-body humanoid setups, and adds a fifth
  axis — *fall-and-recovery safety* — that the pre-humanoid
  version could not address. With ProcessBench-Embodied's
  humanoid split (above), the perturbation-stress and process-
  aware sides of the deployment-credibility eval stack now
  both cover humanoids end-to-end.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the post-training stack acquires its judge

05-01's reading was that the VLA post-training stack had three of its
four cells filled:

1. **Reward**: *AlignRL-VLA* — preference-based action-DPO.
2. **Substrate**: *AcceRL* — asynchronous PPO over many simulators.
3. **Regularizer**: *VLAJS* — annealed VLA prior for cold-start.

The fourth cell — a *judging VLM trained specifically as a robot-
action critic* — landed today as **ActionJudge** (§6). The four
together are now the cleanest end-to-end recipe the field has:

> *VLAJS jump-start → AcceRL substrate → AlignRL preference reward
> with ActionJudge as the preference oracle.*

This recipe (i) does not require hand-engineered rewards, (ii) does
not require synchronous loops, (iii) does not require random init,
and (iv) does not require a frozen general-purpose VLM as judge. The
*next* visible gap is on the data side — the preference pairs
ActionJudge was trained on are still human-labeled, and the
collection cost is the implicit ceiling on the recipe's scale.
Expect the cycle's next push to be a *self-play* preference dataset
generated via WoVR-2-style in-world rollouts, scored by ActionJudge,
and used to fine-tune the next ActionJudge — i.e., the
*self-improving judge* loop. That paper is the obvious next step,
and the AcceRL + WoVR-2 substrate is already in place to run it.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — amortization is the year's design verdict

Three independent papers in 48 hours converge on the same lesson:

- **MemFlow-VLA** (05-01 §3): amortize episodic memory at episode
  boundary, not retrieve as raw trajectories.
- **EpiAmortize** (§3 above): the wrapper that ports the
  amortization recipe to *every* prior memory-VLA.
- **AVA-2** (§8 above): the same factoring transfers from
  manipulation to autonomous driving with no architectural change.

This is the closest the memory-VLA line has come to an architectural
verdict in two years. The reading is that *retrieval over raw
trajectories is a stand-in for a parameter update we did not know how
to schedule*; once we schedule the update at episode boundaries, the
retrieval interface stops being load-bearing. The deeper implication
— mostly speculative — is that the same lesson should apply to the
*world-model* line: persistent latent state (PersistDream §4) is the
amortization view applied to the WM's scene memory rather than to the
policy's task memory. If that reading holds, the world-model and
memory branches are converging on the same factoring, just at
different layers of the stack.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the latent-action vocabulary appears to be real

Two latent-action papers from opposite methodological corners
recovered overlapping primitive sets this cycle:

- **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL** (§5 above): a discrete planner-level
  codebook converges to a 30-entry primitive vocabulary at scale.
- **JALA-2** (05-01 §5): continuous manifold-clustering recovers
  task-family manifolds whose top entries align with the
  DiscreteFlow codebook.

The two methods could not be much more different — one is a
quantizer on a transformer, the other a manifold-alignment loss on
human-video features — and they recover the same primitives. This
is the cleanest evidence yet that the latent-action vocabulary the
field has been chasing since LAPA is a *property of the data*
rather than of any specific architecture. The next interesting
question is whether the recovered vocabulary maps onto the
psycholinguistic primitive set (grasp / push / pour / insert / hand
off / track / align), which would make the latent-action line a
candidate for a true *robot phonology*.

1. **ActionJudge (2605.00059)** — the robot-action critic VLM the
   AlignRL-VLA paper flagged as the missing piece a day earlier,
