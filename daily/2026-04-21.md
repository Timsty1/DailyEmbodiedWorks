# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-04-21)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those I
find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to influence
the near-term research direction. Today's edition focuses on papers
appearing or updated in the **Apr 19–21, 2026** window, with a set of
gap-fills that were queued up during last week's digest and are worth
pulling forward now. Entries already covered in the 04-17 / 04-18 /
04-19 issues are only listed as carried references.

---

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **Goal2Skill: Long-Horizon Manipulation with Adaptive Planning and
  Reflection** (arXiv:2604.13942, updated Apr 20, 2026). A dual-system
  design that is genuinely *closed-loop*, not just a two-stage pipeline:
  a VLM-based planner maintains structured task memory and performs
  goal decomposition, outcome verification, and *error-driven*
  replanning, while a geometry-conditioned diffusion VLA executor
  handles each sub-task. On RMBench long-horizon tasks the method
  reports **32.4%** average success vs **9.8%** for the strongest
  baseline — a 3.3× gap that is almost entirely attributable to the
  reflection/replan loop. The important design choice is that memory,
  verification, and correction are all on the *planner* side; the
  executor remains small and fast. Good counterpoint to single-model
  "giant VLA" designs.

- **ProGAL-VLA: Grounded Alignment through Prospective Reasoning in
  Vision-Language-Action Models** (arXiv:2604.09824). Pushes
  chain-of-thought reasoning *forward in time*: the VLA predicts
  prospective visual states and uses them to anchor action decisions,
  rather than reasoning over the current frame alone. Complements
  WAV's implicit-planning view (see 04-18 §4) from the training-objective
  side.

- **Orion-Lite: Distilling LLM Reasoning into Efficient Vision-Only
  Driving Models** (arXiv:2604.08266). Student replaces the 7B LLM in
  ORION with a lightweight transformer decoder; combination of *latent*
  distillation and ground-truth trajectory supervision lets the 
  student *surpass* its own teacher, with a **150× speedup** on the
  reasoning module, **3× end-to-end** speedup, and **31 GB → 8 GB**
  GPU memory. Shows that for AD the reasoning LLM mostly serves as a
  prior — once distilled into latents, the heavy decoder is redundant.
  Pair with FastDriveVLA (04-19 §8) as the two currently-leading
  "VLA-at-the-edge" recipes for driving.

- **StreamingVLA: Streaming VLA with Action Flow Matching and Adaptive
  Early Observation** (arXiv:2603.28565). Drops action *chunking*
  entirely, learns the trajectory of *action flows*, and streams the
  next action while still observing the scene. Reports **2.4× latency
  speedup** and **6.5×** reduction in execution halting. The
  adaptive-early-observation mechanism is the part I think generalizes
  — it decouples the "I need a decision now" clock from the "I have a
  fresh observation" clock, which is the actual source of edge-device
  stalls.

- *(Carried: R3D (04-19), VGA (04-18), SnapFlow (04-18), A1 (04-18),
  StarVLA, FiS-VLA.)*

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- ★ **SOLE-R1: Video-Language Reasoning as the Sole Reward for On-Robot
  RL** (arXiv:2603.28730). A video-language reasoning model produces
  per-timestep spatiotemporal chain-of-thought reasoning *and* a dense
  task-progress estimate — that single signal is used as the entire
  reward for online RL, replacing hand-designed reward engineering.
  Cleanest execution yet of the "LLM-as-reward" idea for embodied RL:
  the reward is grounded in what the video itself shows, not a
  shortcut through oracle state. Natural companion to the TT-VLA /
  SC-VLA / SimpleVLA-RL triptych from 04-19.

- **FocusVLA: Focused Visual Computation for VLAs** (Mar 2026 digest,
  carried). Diagnoses that current VLA architectures *waste* visual
  token compute on low-information regions; a learned focus head
  re-weights which tokens propagate to the action decoder.
  Unspectacular as an architectural idea but empirically moves
  convergence and dexterity numbers noticeably — the kind of
  incremental fix that is cheap to adopt.

- **ManipArena: Comprehensive Real-World Evaluation of
  Reasoning-Oriented Generalist Robot Manipulation** (arXiv:2603.28545).
  20 reasoning-oriented tasks across 10,812 expert trajectories, with
  the explicit design that evaluation happens on the *physical* robot,
  not a tuned simulator. Exposes the failure modes that appear only
  under real contact, hardware latency, and sensor noise — which is
  where the gap between leaderboard VLAs and deployable VLAs actually
  lives. See §7 for its role in the evaluation stack.

- *(Carried: LangForce/BayesianVLA (04-19), IGAR (04-19), Point-VLA
  (04-19), ICR-Drive (04-18), VLA-Forget (04-18).)*

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- ★ **ChemBot / Skill-VLA: Long-Term Memory for VLA-based Agents in
  Open-World Task Execution** (arXiv:2604.15671, Apr 17, 2026). A
  dual-layer memory architecture where *successful* trajectories are
  consolidated into long-term memory as retrievable assets, and
  short-term memory holds live scene perception and task state. The
  progress-aware Skill-VLA executor is conditioned on both, giving
  the agent genuine "I have done this before" behavior across
  sessions. The domain (chemistry-lab automation) is a stress test
  that forces persistence *across* episodes rather than within one —
  historically the weakest axis of VLA memory work. A clean
  complement to Goal2Skill (§1), which handles *within*-episode
  memory; read them together.

- **Goal2Skill** — see §1. Doubles as a memory paper: the planner's
  structured task memory is what enables adaptive replanning.

- **Critic-in-the-Loop** (arXiv:2603.05185, carried from 04-19).
  Still the cleanest recent proposal for a tri-system memory/value/
  executor split.

- *(Carried: MemER, MemoryVLA, EchoVLA, EEAgent, TrackVLA++, MemGround —
  see 04-17, 04-18, 04-19.)*

## 4. World-Model-Based VLA

- ★ **DriveVLA-W0: World Models Amplify Data Scaling Law in
  Autonomous Driving** (ICLR 2026, arXiv:2510.12796). Uses future-frame
  *world modeling* to provide dense self-supervision on top of sparse
  action labels, and then demonstrates that data-scaling curves for
  VLAs *steepen* once this auxiliary objective is added — i.e.
  world modeling is not just an inductive bias, it is a free
  supervision multiplier. This is the most important scaling-law
  result I have seen for AD-VLA; if it replicates on manipulation
  (GigaBrain-0.5M suggests it does), it changes how we should budget
  real-world data vs. world-model capacity.

- **WMPO: World-Model-based Policy Optimization for VLAs** (workshop /
  tech report, 2026). On-policy GRPO executed entirely inside a
  pixel-space world model whose predictions are aligned to VLA
  features pretrained on web-scale images. The key claim — that
  pixel-aligned world-model rollouts are good enough to replace
  real-env interaction for post-training — is strong; if it holds, it
  removes the largest remaining bottleneck in VLA-RL wall-clock.

- **World-Env: World Model as a Virtual Environment for VLA
  Post-Training** (arXiv:2509.24948). The more conservative cousin of
  WMPO: uses the world model as a safety/cost-reduction layer rather
  than claiming full parity with the real environment, and targets
  the extreme-data-scarcity regime.

- *(Carried: WAV (04-18), Uni-World VLA (04-18), Learning VLA WMs for
  AD (04-18), SC-VLA (04-19), VLA-in-the-Loop (04-19), VLAW, VLA-MBPO.)*

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **LARY: Latent Action Representation Yielding Benchmark for
  Generalizable Vision-to-Action Alignment** (arXiv:2604.11689,
  Apr 13, 2026). First serious benchmark purpose-built for *latent*
  action representations: ~1M videos / 1,000 h across 151 action
  categories, 620K paired images, 595K motion trajectories spanning
  multiple embodiments. Two findings are load-bearing: (i) general
  visual foundation models, trained with *no* action supervision,
  consistently outperform specialized embodied latent-action models;
  (ii) *latent* visual space is better aligned to physical action
  space than pixel space. Together they suggest the "latent
  pretraining on action-free video" line (LAPA, UniVLA, VLA-JEPA,
  WholeBodyVLA) is on the right track — but that the specialization
  step should be lighter than the field currently assumes. This is
  the evaluation infrastructure the latent-action line has been
  missing since LAPA.

- **PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain
  Latent Flow Matching and Robust Tracking** (arXiv:2603.05410).
  Hierarchical multi-"brain" VLA for humanoid whole-body control, with
  semantic-motion intent alignment at the top, efficient flow-matching
  motion generation in the middle, and physics-aware tracking at the
  bottom. **74.9%** average success vs LeVERB's 65.0%. The
  multi-brain split is the first humanoid design I have seen that
  maps cleanly onto the three-loop stack from 04-18.

- **SkillVLA: Tackling Combinatorial Diversity in Dual-Arm
  Manipulation via Skill Reuse** (arXiv:2603.03836). Learns single-arm
  latent skills and recombines them across left/right arm pairings;
  overall task success rises from **0% → 51%**, which is a rare
  "categorical" improvement in the dual-arm literature. Latent skills
  are the *unit of transfer* here, exactly the LARY thesis applied to
  a specific task family.

- *(Carried: VLA-JEPA (04-19), LoLA (04-19), LatBot (04-19), JALA,
  LAPA, UniLACT, UniVLA, Gemini Robotics 1.5 UVMC, WholeBodyVLA.)*

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **EgoScale: Scaling Dexterous Manipulation with Diverse Egocentric
  Human Data** (arXiv:2602.16710). 20,854 h of action-labeled
  egocentric human manipulation video — >20× larger than prior
  efforts — is used to pretrain a VLA, and the resulting
  wrist/hand-action-prediction validation loss follows a clean
  **log-linear scaling law** that correlates with downstream
  real-robot success. Pretraining on 1k → 20k h lifts average task
  completion from **0.30 → 0.71**. This is the first convincing scaling
  law I have seen tied to *action* prediction (not just pixels) on
  *human* data — and the 22-DoF dexterous-hand result transfers down
  to lower-DoF embodiments, which is the right generalization test.
  Sets the upper bound that RL post-training has to beat.

- **SOLE-R1** — see §2. As a reward model for on-robot RL it also
  belongs here; arguably the cleanest "no hand-crafted reward" result
  of the quarter.

- **VLA-R1: Enhancing Reasoning in VLAs** (arXiv:2510.01623, carried).
  RLVR + GRPO optimizing reasoning *and* execution jointly. Reference
  design for the "reasoning-as-RL-signal" pattern.

- *(Carried: TwinRL-VLA (04-19), TT-VLA (04-19), RTC + Mixture of
  Horizons (04-19), ViVa (04-18), SV-VLA (04-18), GigaBrain-0.5M,
  SimpleVLA-RL, VLA-RL, VLA-RFT, RLinf-VLA, IRL-VLA, EvoVLA.)*

## 7. Benchmarks, Simulators & Evaluation

- ★ **ManipArena** (arXiv:2603.28545) — see §2. The argument that
  *real-world* evaluation exposes failure modes that sim-tuned
  leaderboards miss is not new, but ManipArena is the first
  benchmark that executes that argument *at scale*: 20 reasoning
  tasks, 10,812 expert trajectories, physical-robot evaluation.
  Together with VLA-Risk (04-19) and SimX-OR, the evaluation stack
  is consolidating along three orthogonal axes — **reasoning**
  (ManipArena), **physical robustness** (VLA-Risk), and
  **perceptual robustness** (SimX-OR / LIBERO-Plus / LIBERO-Para).

- **LARY** (arXiv:2604.11689) — doubles as the missing latent-action
  benchmark. See §5.

- *(Primary references unchanged: vla-eval, SimX-OR, VLABench,

## Cross-Cutting Observation — memory is becoming the scarce resource

Across the Apr 19–21 batch, the papers that meaningfully extend the
three-loop stack (executor → planner → reflective memory) are the ones
that take **memory** seriously as a first-class substrate:

1. **Within-episode memory** — Goal2Skill's planner memory is what
   lets the loop close. Without structured task memory the "replan on
   failure" trick degrades to "retry-and-hope".
2. **Across-episode memory** — ChemBot/Skill-VLA consolidates
   successful trajectories into retrievable assets. That is the first
   recent VLA design where persistence spans sessions, which is what
   an actually-deployed agent needs.
3. **Latent memory** — LARY argues (and the field is starting to
   agree) that the right representation *for* that memory is latent,
   not pixel-space. VLA-JEPA (04-19) and WholeBodyVLA (04-18) were
   early evidence; LARY is the first benchmark to make the claim
   testable.
4. **World-model memory** — DriveVLA-W0 and WMPO treat the world
   model as a *dynamic* memory of the environment's physics; the
   supervision it produces is dense precisely because physics is
   predictable even when actions are sparse.

The "executor" axis is saturating (SnapFlow at 1 NFE, StreamingVLA at
streaming action flows); the "planner" axis is converging (WAV vs
Critic-in-the-Loop as two dual designs). The open axis — where
near-term gains are largest and recipes are least settled — is how VLAs
represent, retrieve, and update memory.

## Picks of the day (if you only read three)

1. **Goal2Skill (2604.13942)** — cleanest dual-system design with
   genuine reflection/replan memory; **32.4% vs 9.8%** on RMBench is
   the right *kind* of improvement (long-horizon, not
   benchmark-optimized).
2. **LARY (2604.11689)** — first real benchmark for latent actions,
   plus a pointed empirical finding that general visual foundation
   models beat specialized embodied latent-action models. Will shape
   how the field designs latent-action pretraining objectives for at
   least the next year.
3. **ChemBot / Skill-VLA (2604.15671)** — across-episode memory with
   retrievable trajectory assets, evaluated in a domain where
   persistence genuinely matters. The memory paper the field has been
   needing since MemoryVLA.
