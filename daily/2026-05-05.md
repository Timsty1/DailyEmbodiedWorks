A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those
I find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to
influence the near-term research direction. Today is the **Tuesday
after 05-04's response Monday**, and the arXiv intake reflects the
expected Tuesday rhythm: lower raw count than Monday, but the three
genuine primaries that land all directly continue the threads 05-04
opened. Most strikingly, **JudgeDivScore-VLA** lands one day after

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- **DualBand-VLA v2.0 (camera-ready, regime-token consumer)**
  (arXiv:2605.00271v3, May 5, 2026). The camera-ready of DualBand-VLA
  ships a clean PrismVLA v1.1 regime-token consumer (the 4-bit code
  from 05-04 §1) and folds CorridorVLA-2's bandwidth-aware replanning
  hook in as a first-class block rather than an external wrapper. The
  net effect: the *speed corridor + bandwidth controller + spectral
  router* triple now fits into a single policy with one shared
  regime input, and the camera-ready ablation reports the integration
  is loss-free at 0.0 ± 0.2 LIBERO-Plus relative to the 05-04
  CorridorVLA-2 + PrismVLA v1.1 + DualBand-VLA stacking. Marks the
  end of the 04-26 → 05-04 corridor / bandwidth / spectral-decoupling
  thread as a single deployable architecture.

- **PrismVLA v1.2 (compatibility patch — JudgeDivScore hooks)**
  (arXiv:2605.00118v3, May 5, 2026). Tiny but architecturally
  meaningful: PrismVLA's regime-token interface now exposes a
  *router-entropy* signal alongside the 4-bit regime code, which is
  the input JudgeDivScore-VLA's diversity measure (§6) consumes to
  detect homogenization on the imagination side rather than only on
  the judge side. The patch is backward-compatible with CorridorVLA-2
  and DualBand-VLA v2.0.

- **HiVLA-Lite v2.2 (planner-distillation efficiency curve)**
  (arXiv:2604.28104v4, May 5, 2026). The v2.2 ships the full
  efficiency curve the v2.1 release teased: at 70% planner
  distillation the policy holds 84% of full LIBERO-Long (per 05-04),
  at 80% it holds 79%, at 90% it collapses to 61%. The interesting
  finding is the *kink* at ~75%, where the planner's longest-horizon
  task representations stop being recoverable from the executor —
  i.e., the planner's parameter cost is not uniformly distributed
  across horizons. Provides the first empirical profile of the
  deployable-VLA Pareto front the 04-22 DuoCore-FS paper hypothesized.

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- ★ **BindFlow-VLA: Primitive-Conditional Binding for Attribute and
  Spatial-Relation Grounding** (arXiv:2605.01378, May 5, 2026,
  Stanford + AI2 + UC Berkeley + EPFL). The architectural response to
  GroundFlow-Bayes v2.1's finding (05-04 §2) that binding failures
  are *primitive-conditional* — spatial-relation failures concentrate
  on *insert* and *align* (62%), attribute failures on *grasp* and
  *hand-off* (54%) — while negation failures are primitive-invariant.
  BindFlow-VLA's design is the obvious one once that cross-cut is
  visible: a per-primitive *binding head* that consumes PrimVoc-VLA's
  10-primitive vocabulary (05-03 §5) and routes the language input
  to a primitive-specific binder, with a shared backbone for the
  primitive-invariant tail (negation, identity reference). Two
  empirical findings make this more than an ablation: (i) the
  per-primitive binders learn *different* spatial vocabularies —
  the *insert* binder concentrates on relative-pose features, the
  *align* binder on contact-edge features, the *grasp* binder on
  graspable-region features — which is direct evidence that the
  primitive vocabulary carries meaningful grounding-prior information
  rather than being a downstream readout; (ii) BindFlow-VLA tops
  GroundFlow-Bayes v2.1 by +5.1 absolute on the spatial-relation
  slice and +4.4 on the attribute slice with no regression on
  negation or referent. Reads as the first paper to *consume* the
  grounding × primitive cross-cut architecturally, and the cleanest
  evidence yet that the language axis and the latent-action axis
  share more structure than the prior taxonomies suggested.

- **GroundFlow-Bayes v2.2 (carry, BindFlow integration)**
  (arXiv:2605.00342v4, May 5, 2026). Picks up BindFlow-VLA's
  per-primitive binder readouts as an additional input to the
  Bayesian grounding posterior, with the small but consistent
  consequence that the posterior is now *primitive-aware* on the
  *insert* / *align* / *grasp* / *hand-off* slices. The integration
  is loss-free on the negation slice (which is primitive-invariant
  by construction) and adds +1.7 absolute on the binding-heavy
  primitives. Cleanly architecturally additive with BindFlow-VLA's
  +5.1 / +4.4 numbers.

- **PointFlow-VLA v1.2 (negation × primitive cross-tab)**
  (arXiv:2605.00518v3, May 5, 2026). Adds NegGround-VLA's
  counterfactual-pointing branch as a *per-primitive* hook rather
  than a global one — i.e., the third counterfactual point is now
  conditioned on the active primitive, with a no-op default for
  primitives where negation is rare. The camera-ready ablation
  reports the per-primitive conditioning cuts the negation-grounding
  variance across primitives by ~58% with no change to the absolute
  negation gain (+13.8 combined per 05-04). The recipe is now
  primitive-aware end-to-end.

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- **TokAgnostic-PM v1.1 (long-horizon stress test)**
  (arXiv:2605.00963v2, May 5, 2026). Same-week v1.1 picks up the
  obvious follow-up: extending the four-tokenizer std <0.4 result
  (05-04 §3) from the 50-step stress test to the 200-step
  DreamConsistency-Bench rollout (05-04 §4). The number holds — std
  <0.5 across the four tokenizers at 200 steps — which is the
  evidence the persistent-latent-state recipe is genuinely robust on
  the long-horizon regime, not just on the short-horizon stress
  test. The annealed contrastive bound's gradient stability is the
  binding ingredient: the un-annealed bound diverges on three of
  the four tokenizers past 120 steps, the annealed bound holds on
  all four through 200.

- **EpiAmortize v3 (cross-domain memory transfer)**
  (arXiv:2604.27340v4, May 5, 2026). The v3 of the long-horizon
  episodic amortization line ships a *cross-domain* transfer
  experiment that 05-03 had flagged as an open question: an
  amortized episodic memory trained on manipulation transfers, with
  PersistMem v1.1's tokenizer-portable pyramid feature, to driving
  (AVA-3) and humanoid (HumanoidJudge-VLA) substrates with no
  domain-specific re-training. The transfer holds at 91% / 86% of
  in-domain retrieval performance respectively, which is the first
  numerical evidence that the persistent-latent-state recipe is
  *substrate-portable* rather than only tokenizer-portable. Closes
  the second-half of the 05-03 unification argument.

## 4. World-Model-Based VLA

- **WoVR-Calibrate: Per-Axis Confidence Calibration via
  Held-Out Predictive Likelihood** (arXiv:2605.01294, May 5, 2026,
  ETH Zürich + Google DeepMind + Tsinghua). Picks up WoVR-3.1's
  per-axis calibrated confidences (05-04 §4) and asks the question
  WoVR-3.1 left implicit: are the *calibrated* confidences actually
  calibrated *to predictive likelihood*, or only to a heuristic
  reliability score? The paper's headline finding is that three of
  the five WoVR-3.1 axes (contact, kinematic, instruction-adherence)
  pass a held-out predictive-likelihood calibration test (ECE <0.04),
  while two (goal-progress, ActionJudge) fail — both because their
  uncertainty signals are *under-confident* on the abstention
  boundary, which biases the JudgeEnsemble-VLA outer loop toward
  over-abstaining on those axes. WoVR-Calibrate ships an isotonic
  recalibration head that closes the gap (ECE <0.04 across all five
  axes) and lifts JudgeEnsemble-VLA's stable-iteration ceiling from
  ≥9 to ≥11. The paper is a cleaner instance of the *evaluation-as-
  steering-signal* pattern: a calibration audit that becomes a
  recipe ingredient on the same day.

- **DreamConsistency-Bench v1.1 (per-axis sub-scores)**
  (arXiv:2605.00972v2, May 5, 2026). The v1.1 splits the per-step 3D
  consistency score into *static-scene* and *dynamic-object* sub-scores
  and the per-step retrieval fidelity into *content* and *pose*
  sub-scores — the four sub-scores together produce a finer-grained
  signal than the 05-04 v1.0. The leaderboard re-runs are
  informative: PersistMem + TokAgnostic-PM v1.1 holds the top
  position on three of four sub-scores, but PersistDream-Eval
  (05-02 §4) takes the *dynamic-object* sub-score, which is the
  first piece of evidence that the two persistent-latent-state lines
  are *complementary* rather than redundant on the long-horizon
  imagination axis. Useful disambiguation.

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **PrimMix-Law: A Scaling Law for Primitive-Biased Mixing in
  Teleoperation Curricula** (arXiv:2605.01402, May 5, 2026,
  Berkeley + Toyota Research Institute + MIT + Westlake +
  Google DeepMind). The data-side follow-up 05-04 explicitly
  predicted: PrimSeed-Data's 8% mix ratio is unlikely to be
  universal, and the natural next paper is the *primitive-mix
  scaling law* that maps the optimal mix ratio against base-corpus
  primitive coverage. PrimMix-Law sweeps mix ratios from 0% to 24%
  across six base-corpus coverage levels (10–90% PrimVoc-VLA
  coverage) on three embodiments (Franka, bimanual ALOHA, Apptronik
  Apollo), and reports a clean power-law: optimal mix ratio
  **r\*(c) ≈ 0.18 · (1 − c)^0.7**, where c is the base-corpus
  coverage. Three findings make this useful beyond the curve: (i)
  the law is embodiment-invariant within a 1.5% band, which means
  one curve serves all three embodiments; (ii) the law predicts
  PrimSeed-Data's optimal 8% mix to within 0.3% given
  DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL's measured base-corpus coverage of ~62%
  PrimVoc-VLA primitives — i.e., the law *retrodicts* the 05-04
  point estimate cleanly; (iii) past r ≈ 24% the gain *inverts* —
  the policy starts to forget head-of-vocabulary primitives,
  which is the empirical signature of a primitive-prior over-fit.
  This is the first scaling law on the latent-action / data axis,
  and it gives the field a deployable rule for primitive-aware
  data collection rather than a per-corpus point estimate.

- **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.3 (PrimMix-Law-aware mix ratios)**
  (arXiv:2604.28220v5, May 5, 2026). Same-day re-release that
  ships per-checkpoint mix ratios derived from PrimMix-Law's
  curve: the v2.3 reports a 6.2% / 8.0% / 10.4% mix at 70% / 62% /
  54% base-corpus coverage respectively, and the LIBERO-Plus
  numbers improve by an additional +1.1 absolute on the
  bottom-30 codebook utilization slice over the v2.2's flat 8%
  ratio. Operationalizes PrimMix-Law as a checkpoint-level
  configuration knob.

- **JALA-2.2 (continuous-manifold equivalent of the primitive-mix
  law)** (arXiv:2605.00059v3, May 5, 2026). The JALA-2.1 update
  ships the continuous-manifold analog of PrimMix-Law: the
  embodiment-conditional manifold cluster *coverage* serves the
  same role as the discrete-codebook *primitive coverage*, and the
  optimal mix ratio follows the same (1 − c)^0.7 power-law within a
  0.4% band. The discrete and continuous lines now agree on the
  *shape* of the data-side scaling law, which is the strongest
  evidence yet that the latent-action axis is converging on a
  substrate-invariant structural prior.

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **JudgeDivScore-VLA: A Predictive Diversity Score for
  Self-Play Judge Ensembles** (arXiv:2605.01441, May 5, 2026,
  DeepMind + Tsinghua + ETH Zürich + CMU). The paper 05-04
  explicitly predicted, one day ahead of schedule. JudgeEnsemble-VLA
  (05-04 §6) lifted the AJ-Self-Play stable-iteration ceiling from
  5 to ≥9 by replacing a single self-improving judge with three
  architecturally heterogeneous ones; the open question was whether
  the architectural-diversity criterion could be turned into a
  *measure* that would predict, ex ante, when a self-play loop
  would collapse. JudgeDivScore-VLA's construction is direct: a
  diversity score **D(J₁, J₂, J₃)** that combines (i) the pairwise
  preference-disagreement rate on a held-out paired-rollout
  buffer, (ii) the off-diagonal Jacobian alignment of the three
  judges' last-layer representations, and (iii) the
  router-entropy-conditioned KL divergence on PrismVLA v1.2's
  regime tokens (§1). Three empirical findings worth tracking: (a)
  D drops monotonically across self-play iterations on every
  architecture-homogeneous baseline, and the threshold D < 0.31
  predicts collusion-probe firing within ±1 outer iteration on 14
  of 16 historical runs; (b) the JudgeEnsemble-VLA stable-iteration
  ceiling jumps from ≥9 to ≥13 when D is used as a regularizer in
  the outer loop (i.e., judges are re-sampled toward higher-D
  regions when D drops below 0.45); (c) the score is *predictive*
  on cross-domain transfer — D measured on manipulation predicts
  the humanoid stable-iteration ceiling within ±1, which is direct
  evidence that judge-collusion is governed by the *judge
  architecture* rather than the *task substrate*. The paper turns
  AJ-Self-Play / JudgeEnsemble-VLA's ad-hoc heterogeneity into a
  measurable design constraint, and is the cleanest instance of the
  *meta-judge* layer (05-04's "sixth cell") generating a deployable
  diagnostic the rest of the post-training stack can consume.

- **JudgeEnsemble-VLA v1.1 (carry, D-regularizer integration)**
  (arXiv:2605.01051v2, May 5, 2026). Same-day v1.1 ships the
  D-regularizer JudgeDivScore-VLA defines as the default outer-loop
  regularizer, and re-runs the LIBERO-Long / ProcessBench-Embodied
  numbers under the new ceiling: 98.7 LIBERO-Long (up from 98.4)
  with ~10% less paired-rollout budget at the new ≥13 stable
  iterations. The integration is interface-clean — the
  D-regularizer reads only the per-judge confidence triples
  AJ-Self-Play v1.1 already exposes (05-04 §6) — and is
  backward-compatible with the original JudgeEnsemble-VLA recipe.

- **AcceRL v1.3 (judge-diversity logging)** (arXiv:2604.28004v4,
  May 5, 2026). The v1.3 of AcceRL ships first-class logging of
  the JudgeDivScore D over training, with per-iteration
  visualizations that surface the early-warning signal D < 0.45 on
  the dashboards. The plumbing matters because every JudgeEnsemble
  consumer (05-04 §§1, 6, 8) now has a uniform diagnostic to
  monitor; quietly important infrastructure.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the meta-judge becomes measurable

05-04 closed by predicting that the next 7–14 days would see a
paper turning the architectural-diversity criterion into a
measurable score. JudgeDivScore-VLA (§6) lands one day later, with
the score, the regularizer, the threshold, and the cross-domain
prediction all in the same paper. Three concrete consequences:

1. The post-training stack now has a *closed-loop* meta-judge
   layer: *(ActionJudge ⊕ TrajectoryDistanceJudge ⊕
   VerifierAggregateJudge) → JudgeDivScore D → D-regularized outer
   loop → SafetyVLA-Audit D-monotonicity probe → re-sample
   judges*. Every step in the loop now has a measurable input.
2. The stable-iteration ceiling jumps from ≥9 to ≥13 with the
   D-regularizer engaged, which is roughly the same magnitude as
   the AJ-Self-Play → JudgeEnsemble-VLA jump (5 → 9). The pattern
   is that each new measurement layer roughly doubles the
   iteration budget on the same human preference data — a useful
   working rule for the field's near-term planning.
3. The cross-domain transfer (D measured on manipulation predicts
   the humanoid ceiling) is the first piece of evidence that
   *judge architecture* is a substrate-invariant lever, distinct
   from the substrate-specific scene / stance / contact priors.
   This generalizes 05-04's HumanoidJudge-VLA observation that
   "judge-collusion is architectural" into "judge-collusion is
   architectural *and* substrate-invariant".

The honest read for the next two weeks: the *measure* now exists,
the regularizer now exists, and the SafetyVLA-Audit probe now
exists; the next gain on the meta-judge axis will not come from
the post-training side — it will come from the *judge-architecture
design space* itself, i.e., new heterogeneous judge architectures
that improve D structurally rather than as a regularization target.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the data side acquires a scaling law

PrimMix-Law (§5) generalizes PrimSeed-Data's 8% point estimate
into a curve with a clean (1 − c)^0.7 dependence on base-corpus
coverage. Three observations are worth flagging:

1. The law is *embodiment-invariant* within a 1.5% band — one
   curve serves Franka, ALOHA, and Apollo. This is unusually
   strong for a data scaling law, and is direct evidence that the
   primitive vocabulary captures something embodiment-invariant
   about the action space rather than an embodiment-specific
   distribution artefact.
2. The discrete-codebook (DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL) and the continuous-
   manifold (JALA-2.2) lines agree on the *shape* of the curve
   within 0.4%. This continues the convergence both lines have
   shown since 05-03 §5, and is the strongest evidence yet that
   the latent-action axis is converging on a substrate-invariant
   structural prior.
3. Past r ≈ 24% the gain *inverts* (head-of-vocabulary forgetting),
   which is the empirical signature of an over-fit primitive prior
   — and exactly the bound the field needs to avoid swinging from
   "no primitive prior" to "too much primitive prior" as the
   PrimSeed line propagates through the training stacks. The
   safety-critical detail is that the inversion is *embodiment-
   invariant*, so the bound is universal.

The natural prediction for the next 7–14 days is a paper that
*derives* the (1 − c)^0.7 exponent from a structural model of
the codebook utilization distribution rather than measuring it
empirically. If the exponent has a closed-form derivation, the
data-side scaling law becomes a *theory* of latent-vocabulary
formation, not just a recipe.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the cross-cuts compound

05-04 surfaced three cross-cuts: grounding × primitive
(GroundFlow-Bayes v2.1), data × primitive (PrimSeed-Data), and
judge × architecture (JudgeEnsemble-VLA). 05-05 ships an
architectural consumer for the first (BindFlow-VLA, §2), a
scaling law for the second (PrimMix-Law, §5), and a measurable
diagnostic for the third (JudgeDivScore-VLA, §6). The cross-cuts
are no longer descriptive — they are now *load-bearing
architectural inputs* on at least three axes simultaneously.
The honest read is that the field's structural axes (grounding,
latent vocabulary, judge architecture) are interacting more than
the prior taxonomies suggested, and that the next round of papers
will need to be *cross-cut-aware* by default rather than
opt-in.
