# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-05-07)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those
I find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to
influence the near-term research direction. Today is the **Thursday
after 05-06's theory Wednesday**, and the arXiv intake has the
empirical-follow-through character one would expect: the absolute
count is up, and the three primaries that land are the *empirical
consumers* of yesterday's theory papers rather than further theory.
**FourJudge-VLA** is the four-judge-committee paper 05-06 predicted
on the meta-judge axis, with a non-bilinear implicit-reward judge that
lifts the structural D bound from 0.49 to 0.63 and the stable-iteration
ceiling from ≥18 to ≥24. **PrimMix-Dynamics** is the training-time
extension of PrimMix-Theory 05-06 also explicitly named: a derivation
of how the Zipfian tail exponent α evolves under joint cross-cut
training, with the empirically critical finding that α is preserved
to within 0.02 under CrossCut-VLA's three-block schedule but drifts
to 1.21 under naive single-axis fine-tuning — i.e., the data-side
theory is preserved by the architecture-side cross-cut design *and
only* by it. The third primary, **PrimCond-Suite**, ships a unified
toolbox that exposes the per-primitive conditional interface across
grounding, memory, world-model, and judge modules — the first paper
to treat 05-06's "primitive-conditional turn" as a deployable
substrate rather than a per-axis upgrade. The day reads as the
field's *empirical Thursday*: yesterday's theory papers are already
producing both the predicted next-layer architectures and the
infrastructure that lets them compose.

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **PrimCond-Suite: A Unified Primitive-Conditional Substrate for
  Cross-Cut-Aware VLA** (arXiv:2605.02014, May 7, 2026, Stanford +
  CMU + Tsinghua + Westlake + DeepMind). The infrastructure paper
  the field has needed since the primitive-conditional turn started
  generalizing across axes one week ago. PrimCond-Suite exposes
  *one* per-primitive conditional interface that grounding
  (BindFlow-VLA), memory (PersistMem v1.2), world-model
  (WoVR-Calibrate v1.1), and judge (HeteroJudge-VLA) modules read
  through, with three load-bearing design choices: (i) the
  primitive-vocabulary key is shared across modules and pinned to
  PrimVoc-VLA's K = 384 codebook, so that an *insert* binding-head
  slot, an *insert* memory slot, an *insert* dream-consistency
  bucket, and an *insert* judge-recalibration cell are all addressed
  by the same opaque key; (ii) the per-primitive conditional payload
  is a fixed 64-dimensional descriptor that each consumer module
  *projects* into its own latent space, which avoids the "every
  module re-parametrizes the primitive" failure mode that 05-06's
  per-primitive variants individually paid for; (iii) the suite
  exposes a *primitive-coverage matrix* (modules × primitives) that
  reports which modules have a per-primitive specialization for
  which primitives, with a default-fallback path for cells the
  caller has not specialized. Two empirical findings make this
  more than a refactor: (a) re-running CrossCut-VLA (05-06 §1)
  through the suite recovers all of yesterday's gains *and*
  closes the residual non-decomposability gap of +1.4 absolute
  to +0.3 — i.e., the residual coupling that no two-block ablation
  could explain becomes individually attributable once the
  primitive descriptor is shared; (b) modules that adopt the
  suite's interface gain 0.4–0.7 absolute on average across
  LIBERO-Plus / LIBERO-Long / DreamConsistency-Bench v1.2 with no
  retraining of the underlying policies, simply by aligning the
  primitive keys. Reads as the *substrate paper* of the cycle
  the way HeteroJudge-VLA was the structural paper of last week's
  cycle, and is the first piece of infrastructure that consumes
  the primitive vocabulary as a *field-wide coordinate system*
  rather than a per-paper choice.

- **CrossCut-VLA v1.1 (PrimCond-Suite integration)**
  (arXiv:2605.01612v2, May 7, 2026). Same-week v1.1 of yesterday's
  flagship architecture re-implements its three coupled blocks on
  PrimCond-Suite's interface. The interesting empirical detail:
  the v1.1 *removes* the bespoke regime-token shared across the
  three blocks (05-06 §1) in favor of the suite's primitive
  descriptor, and the coupling gain that v1.0 reported as +1.4
  absolute *non-decomposable* is now decomposed into +1.1 from
  the primitive-descriptor sharing and +0.3 from a residual
  cross-cut coupling. The architectural read is that the
  non-decomposability finding 05-06 highlighted was largely
  *primitive-descriptor sharing in disguise*, which makes the
  cross-cut gain look more interpretable — and points to the
  primitive vocabulary as the field's substrate-level coupling
  channel.

- **DualBand-VLA v2.2 (PrimCond-Suite-aware bandwidth router)**
  (arXiv:2605.00271v5, May 7, 2026). The bandwidth controller now
  consumes PrimCond-Suite's primitive descriptor on top of

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- **BindFlow-VLA v1.2 (PrimCond-Suite consumer)**
  (arXiv:2605.01378v3, May 7, 2026). The binding head now reads
  PrimCond-Suite's 64-dimensional primitive descriptor instead of
  PrimVoc-VLA's raw codebook key, and exposes the per-primitive
  contact prior (05-06 §2) as a suite-registered payload that
  CrossCut-VLA v1.1 and PersistMem v1.3 both consume. The
  empirical finding worth flagging: the *primitive-pair*
  binding cells where GroundFlow-Bayes v2.3 (05-06 §2) introduced
  the product-form posterior gain an additional +0.6 absolute
  when the binders are addressed through the shared descriptor —
  the same coupling-via-shared-key pattern §1's non-decomposability
  decomposition revealed.

- **NegBind-VLA: A Primitive-Invariant Negation Head with
  Compositional Coverage** (arXiv:2605.02103, May 7, 2026,
  AI2 + Princeton + University of Tokyo). Picks up the
  *negation × hand-off × spatial-relation* failure cell
  ProcessBench-Embodied v0.6 (05-06 §7) flagged as the
  remaining open problem on the language-grounding side. The
  design exploits the empirical fact (GroundFlow-Bayes v2.1,
  05-04 §2) that negation failures are *primitive-invariant* —
  i.e., the binding head conditions on primitives but the
  negation head should not — and routes the negation signal
  through a primitive-marginalized path with explicit
  composition over the *hand-off × spatial-relation* axis.
  The cell ProcessBench-Embodied flagged drops from 71% of the
  residual mass to 38%, which is the largest single-cell move
  on the cross-tab benchmark since v0.5. A clean, narrowly
  scoped paper that closes one of the four field-wide open cells.

- **GroundFlow-Bayes v2.4 (suite-aware compositional priors)**
  (arXiv:2605.00342v6, May 7, 2026). The product-form posterior
  for primitive-pair instructions (05-06 §2) is re-expressed as a
  PrimCond-Suite *payload composition* operator, which means a
  caller asking for the binding posterior on a *grasp-and-insert*
  instruction now reads the composed payload through the suite
  rather than constructing it locally. The release also extends
  the compositionality finding to *primitive-triples* (e.g.,
  *grasp-rotate-insert*), with a small but consistent +0.4
  absolute gain on the triple-pair cells of LIBERO-Long.

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- **PersistMem v1.3 (suite-aligned per-primitive retrieval)**
  (arXiv:2604.27870v5, May 7, 2026). The v1.3 re-implements the
  per-primitive retrieval head v1.2 introduced (05-06 §3) as a
  PrimCond-Suite slot, and extends the long-horizon
  *primitive-pair* slice gain from +2.4 absolute to +3.1 by
  composing the retrieval payloads in the suite-native way
  GroundFlow-Bayes v2.4 (§2) does for the binding posteriors.
  The composition operator is reused unchanged across modules
  — the cleanest evidence to date that the primitive descriptor
  is functioning as a *shared algebra* rather than a per-module
  convention.

- **HorizonProbe-VLA: A Diagnostic for Tokenizer-Specific Drift
  Beyond 250 Steps** (arXiv:2605.02230, May 7, 2026, Berkeley +
  ETH Zürich + AI2). Picks up TokAgnostic-PM v1.2's finding
  (05-06 §3) that the four-tokenizer std rises from <0.5 at 200
  steps to 0.7 at 300 steps with one tokenizer (VQ-VAE-256)
  drifting visibly past 250. HorizonProbe-VLA is a per-step
  drift-attribution diagnostic that decomposes the std rise into
  three sources: (i) *lossy-codebook* drift from token granularity
  (62%), (ii) *positional-extrapolation* drift from the rotary
  scheme (24%), and (iii) *attention-sink* drift from the latent
  state's residual-stream geometry (14%). The empirical bottom
  line is that the 250-step ceiling 05-06 surfaced is *codebook-
  dominated* rather than memory-dominated, which makes the next
  long-horizon gain a tokenizer-side problem (PrimMix-Theory's
  codebook-curation toolkit, §5) rather than a memory-side one.
  Clean diagnostic that turns yesterday's empirical kink into a
  prescriptive recipe.

- **EpiAmortize v3.1 (suite-registered cross-domain head)**
  (arXiv:2604.28301v3, May 7, 2026). The cross-domain retrieval
  benchmark head EpiAmortize ships (05-02–05 §3) registers
  through PrimCond-Suite as the *cross-domain* payload, with the
  per-primitive retrieval slots (PersistMem v1.3) and the
  cross-domain payload composing through the same suite operator.
  The composition recovers an additional +0.7 absolute on the
  EpiAmortize cross-domain × primitive-pair cell over PersistMem
  v1.3 alone.

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **PrimMix-Dynamics: Training-Time Evolution of the Zipfian
  Tail Exponent under Joint Cross-Cut Training**
  (arXiv:2605.02281, May 7, 2026, Berkeley + Westlake + ETH
  Zürich + Toyota Research Institute). The training-time
  extension of PrimMix-Theory 05-06 explicitly named, on
  schedule. PrimMix-Theory's (1 − c)^0.7 scaling law assumed
  the codebook frequency exponent α ≈ 1.4 was a static feature
  of the latent vocabulary; PrimMix-Dynamics asks whether α is
  preserved under joint training of the architecture's three
  cross-cut blocks (CrossCut-VLA, 05-06 §1). The construction
  derives α(t) under a stochastic-gradient model in which the
  codebook's head and tail are subject to *differential* loss
  gradients: the head receives the language-head's
  cross-entropy gradient at scale ∝ c, while the tail receives
  the data-sampler's coverage gradient at scale ∝ (1 − c) ·
  PrimMix-Law's c-dependent mix ratio. The closed-form
  prediction is that α(t) is preserved iff the head and tail
  gradient norms are balanced to within a factor 1 + 1/(α − 1)
  ≈ 3.5; otherwise α drifts toward whichever side is
  over-weighted. Three empirical findings make this load-bearing:
  (a) under CrossCut-VLA's three-block schedule, α is preserved
  at 1.40 ± 0.02 over 1.2M steps — i.e., the architecture-side
  cross-cut design preserves the data-side theory's structural
  invariant *by construction* through the balance of the
  language-head and data-sampler gradients (the paper
  decomposes this and finds the ratio sits at 3.4 ± 0.4); (b)
  under naive single-axis fine-tuning (language-head only,
  no data-sampler), α drifts from 1.40 to 1.21 within 200K
  steps — the head-of-vocabulary gradient overwhelms the
  tail-coverage gradient, the Zipfian tail flattens, and the
  (1 − c)^0.7 curve becomes time-varying as 05-06 had warned;
  (c) the α drift is *predictively* tied to LIBERO-Plus
  bottom-30 codebook utilization regression: every 0.05 of α
  drift corresponds to ~0.6 absolute regression on that slice,
  retrodicting all 12 measured fine-tuning runs within ±0.4
  absolute. The paper's structural conclusion is that the
  cross-cut-aware architecture is a *necessary* condition for
  the static-α theory to hold under training, not just a
  helpful one — i.e., the (1 − c)^0.7 scaling law does not
  survive single-axis training, and the cross-cut design is
  exactly the architecture that preserves it. PrimMix-Dynamics
  closes one of the field's tightest theory–architecture
  loops in recent memory.

- **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.5 (Dynamics-aware codebook regularizer)**
  (arXiv:2604.28220v7, May 7, 2026). Picks up PrimMix-Dynamics's
  3.5-fold gradient-balance condition as an explicit
  *codebook-frequency regularizer* on the head/tail partition,
  which keeps α at 1.40 ± 0.01 under the v2.5's continued
  training over a fresh ALOHA corpus. The LIBERO-Plus
  bottom-30 codebook utilization slice gains an additional +0.7
  absolute over v2.4, and the regression on the head-of-vocabulary
  forgetting bound improves by 1.1 absolute relative — i.e., the
  prescription PrimMix-Theory promised at the codebook-design
  layer (05-06 §5) and PrimMix-Dynamics promised at the
  training-schedule layer is now visibly cumulative.

- **JALA-2.4 (continuous-manifold Dynamics consumer)**
  (arXiv:2605.00059v5, May 7, 2026). The v2.4 of JALA exposes
  an analogous *cluster-density gradient regularizer* on the
  continuous-manifold side, with the manifold cluster
  cardinality playing the role of the codebook head/tail
  partition. The continuous-manifold α is held at 1.40 ± 0.02
  under joint training, in line with the discrete-codebook
  theory. The discrete–continuous convergence is now confirmed
  on the *training-time dynamics* axis as well as the static
  scaling axis.

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **FourJudge-VLA: A Four-Judge Committee with a Non-Bilinear
  Implicit-Reward Head** (arXiv:2605.02360, May 7, 2026, DeepMind
  + Stanford + EPFL + Tsinghua + Toyota Research Institute). The
  paper 05-06 explicitly predicted on the meta-judge axis. The
  construction adds a fourth judge (J₄) — a *non-bilinear
  implicit-reward* head with a low-rank tensor utility instead of
  HeteroJudge-VLA's bilinear utility (05-06 §6) — to the existing
  three-judge committee, with two design moves that make the
  addition load-bearing: (i) the tensor utility is rank-3 and
  consumes (state, action, primitive) jointly rather than the
  bilinear (state, action) used in J₃, which lifts the
  decision-theoretic basis dimensionality from 3 to 4 in the
  precise sense the field's preference-signal decomposition
  literature uses; (ii) the four pairwise Jacobian-alignment
  bounds are computed in closed form and reported as a 4 × 4
  table whose worst entry is 0.71 (J₃ ↔ J₄, the two implicit-
  reward heads, by far the closest pair) and whose best is 0.51
  (J₂ ↔ J₄), giving a structural lower bound on the four-judge
  D of 0.63 — up from the three-judge bound of 0.49. Three
  empirical findings make this the cleanest single-paper
  meta-judge gain since HeteroJudge-VLA: (a) FourJudge-VLA's D
  never drops below 0.61 on any of 31 self-play runs, and the
  lower bound is tight at 0.63 ± 0.02; (b) the JudgeEnsemble-VLA
  stable-iteration ceiling jumps from ≥18 to ≥24, the largest
  single-paper jump on this axis since the line opened, and the
  v1.3 release's measured doubling slope finally bends — the
  ceiling rise from 0.49 → 0.63 (D bound) corresponds to
  ≥18 → ≥24 (ceiling) rather than the ≥18 → ≥36 the
  doubling-per-layer pattern would have predicted, which is the
  first evidence that the meta-judge ladder is decelerating; (c)
  the non-bilinear J₄ contributes the largest marginal gain on
  the *primitive-conditional* preference signal — runs where J₄
  is ablated lose 1.8 absolute on LIBERO-Long primitive-pair
  cells while losing only 0.3 on singleton cells, which means
  the non-bilinear utility is doing primitive-compositional work
  the bilinear judges cannot. Reads as the meta-judge axis's
  *next-layer* paper after HeteroJudge-VLA, with both the
  predicted ceiling jump and the predicted deceleration on the
  doubling slope.

- **JudgeEnsemble-VLA v1.3 (FourJudge default)**
  (arXiv:2605.01051v4, May 7, 2026). Same-day v1.3 ships the
  four-judge configuration as the new default, with the
  three-judge configuration retained as a fallback for legacy
  deployments. The LIBERO-Long number rises to 99.4 (up from
  v1.2's 99.1), with another ~10% reduction in paired-rollout
  budget at the new ≥24 stable iterations. The integration is
  again cleanly minimal — the v1.3 reads FourJudge's four
  judges through PrimCond-Suite's primitive descriptor (§1),
  which means a single suite-aware policy can read the
  per-primitive judge contributions through the same interface
  it reads the per-primitive binding heads (§2) and the
  per-primitive memory slots (§3).

- **AcceRL v1.5 (FourJudge dashboards)**
  (arXiv:2604.28004v6, May 7, 2026). The per-judge
  architectural-bias logging v1.4 introduced (05-06 §6) is
  extended to the four-judge committee, with a new 4 × 4
  pairwise-Jacobian heatmap that surfaces which judge pair is
  rate-limiting on any given iteration. The first piece of
  meta-judge infrastructure that visualizes the *closeness* of
  the J₃ ↔ J₄ pair (the 0.71 bound) as a deployment signal —
  i.e., a deployer who sees J₃ and J₄ tracking too close on
  their dashboard knows to scale the J₄ rank or rotate
  preference signals.

## 7. Benchmarks, Simulators & Evaluation

- **LBM Eval v3.2 — primitive-coverage axis**
  (leaderboard report, May 7, 2026). The v3.2 adds a
  *primitive-coverage* axis to the cross-cut leaderboard
  (05-06 §7), reporting for each entry which of PrimVoc-VLA's
  K = 384 primitives it has specialized for across modules. The
  immediate consequence is a coverage-vs-depth scatter that
  surfaces three distinct deployment profiles: (i) *broad &
  shallow* (CrossCut-VLA v1.1 covers ≥ 280 primitives at
  shallow specialization), (ii) *narrow & deep* (PrismVLA v1.2
  covers ~80 primitives at deep specialization), and (iii)
  *suite-aware composite* (PrimCond-Suite-registered policies
  cover ≥ 320 primitives at deep specialization through the
  shared descriptor). The leaderboard now scores both axes
  jointly, and the suite-aware composite tops the joint
  ranking, which validates §1's substrate-paper framing.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the primitive vocabulary becomes a shared algebra

05-06 closed by predicting that the primitive-conditional turn
would generalize from grounding to memory and world-model on the
same day, and that the field would acquire a substrate-invariant
coordinate system. PrimCond-Suite (§1) realizes that prediction
by exposing *one* per-primitive interface across grounding,
memory, world-model, and judge modules, with three concrete
consequences:

1. The non-decomposability finding 05-06 highlighted as the
   first quantitative evidence of multiplicative cross-cut
   compounding (+1.4 absolute) is *largely* decomposed once
   the primitive descriptor is shared (CrossCut-VLA v1.1, §1).
   The honest read is that yesterday's coupling gain was
   primarily primitive-descriptor sharing in disguise, with
   ~0.3 absolute residual coupling that survives. The
   field's structural coupling channel is the primitive
   vocabulary itself.

2. The suite-aware composite tops the LBM Eval v3.2 leaderboard
   (§7) on both coverage and depth axes, and the
   *suite-registered* version of every consumer module (§§2–6
   v1.x releases) gains 0.4–0.7 absolute on average without
   retraining the underlying policies. The composability
   premium is real and consistent across modules — i.e., the
   primitive vocabulary is functioning as a *shared algebra*,
   not just a shared label.

3. The static-scene × identity-reference cell on
   DreamConsistency-Bench v1.3 (§4) loses 0.3 absolute under
   the suite, which is the first cross-axis trade-off the
   substrate has surfaced. The honest cost: not every cell
   benefits from the shared coordinate system, and the
   substrate's failure modes are now visible on the
   leaderboard.

The natural prediction for the next 7–14 days is a paper that
*designs* the primitive vocabulary jointly with the suite — i.e.,
PrimVoc-VLA v2 with K, α, and the per-module specialization
profile chosen to minimize the static-scene × identity-reference
trade-off and maximize the cross-axis composability. The current
PrimVoc-VLA was designed as a discrete-codebook artefact; the
next iteration will likely be designed as a substrate-level
coordinate system from the start.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the meta-judge ladder decelerates

FourJudge-VLA (§6) lifts the stable-iteration ceiling from ≥18
to ≥24 with a closed-form D ≥ 0.63 from a four-judge committee
whose fourth judge is non-bilinear in the precise sense the
preference-signal decomposition literature requires. Three
observations:

1. The doubling-per-layer pattern 05-05 named (≥9 → ≥13 →
   ≥18) finally bends: ≥18 → ≥24 is a 1.33× ratio rather than
   1.4×, which is the first evidence that the meta-judge
   ladder is decelerating. The information-theoretic reading is
   that the four-judge committee saturates the
   decision-theoretic basis at rank 4, and the next layer
   would require a *non-decomposable* preference signal
   (e.g., a context-conditional utility that does not factor
   over (state, action, primitive)) to extend the basis
   further.

2. The non-bilinear J₄'s largest marginal gain is on the
   *primitive-conditional* slice (§6), which is the cleanest
   evidence that the meta-judge axis is now coupled to the
   primitive vocabulary in the same way the grounding,
   memory, and world-model axes already are. The four
   structural axes that 05-04–06 surfaced as substrate-
   invariant coordinates are now *jointly* substrate-invariant
   under the PrimCond-Suite interface.

3. The scale-dependent diversity-decay pattern SafetyVLA-Audit
   v3.4 (§8) surfaces sharpens at the 4B threshold rather
   than the 8B threshold of the three-judge audit — i.e.,
   the four-judge configuration *raises* the minimum
   substrate scale at which the structural diversity holds,
   which is a deployment caveat the field will need to
   internalize before the four-judge default propagates to
   smaller-substrate releases.

The honest read for the next two weeks: the meta-judge ladder
is decelerating, the next gain will require a non-decomposable
preference signal rather than another judge, and the smaller-
substrate community will need explicit architectural
compensation if it wants to track the four-judge ceiling.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the data-side theory survives only under the cross-cut architecture

PrimMix-Dynamics (§5) closes a tight loop: PrimMix-Theory's
(1 − c)^0.7 scaling law is preserved iff the cross-cut
architecture's language-head and data-sampler gradients are
balanced to within 1 + 1/(α − 1) ≈ 3.5, which is the design
CrossCut-VLA realizes. Three observations:

1. The cross-cut architecture is no longer a "helpful" choice
   for the data-side scaling law — it is a *necessary* one.
   Naive single-axis fine-tuning drifts α from 1.40 to 1.21
   within 200K steps, the (1 − c)^0.7 curve becomes
   time-varying, and the LIBERO-Plus bottom-30 codebook
   utilization regresses by 2.3 absolute. The architecture
   side and the data side are now visibly *coupled* at the
   theoretical level, not just the empirical level.

2. The 3.5-fold gradient-balance condition is *prescriptive*:
   DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.5 and JALA-2.4 both ship explicit
   regularizers that hold α at 1.40 ± 0.01–0.02 under
   continued training, with cumulative gains over the static
   PrimMix-Theory regime. The data-side theory is now
   training-time-stable by design.

3. The discrete–continuous convergence is now confirmed on
   the dynamics axis (JALA-2.4) as well as the static axis,
   which means the field's two latent-action substrates are
   theoretically aligned at three layers (codebook design,
   inversion threshold, training-time evolution). The
   substrate-invariance of the latent-action axis is becoming
   one of the cleanest structural facts in the field.
