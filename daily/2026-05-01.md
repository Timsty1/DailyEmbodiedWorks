# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-05-01)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those I
find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to influence
the near-term research direction. Today's edition is a *month-end cap*
on the **Apr 28 – May 1, 2026** window: arXiv intake picks back up after
the Apr 25–27 slowdown, and the cycle is dominated by three threads —
(a) post-training *alignment-as-RL* recipes that try to fix the
"VLAs-don't-explore" problem first surfaced by VLAJS; (b) a small but
unusually coherent batch of **VLA-with-memory** papers that finally
agree on the shape of the retrieval interface; and (c) two
benchmark releases that target what *How VLAs (Really) Work*
(04-26 §7) called the metric blind-spot. The thematic story this
month-end is *closing the loop end-to-end*: where the Apr 21–27
batch identified the failure modes, the Apr 28–May 1 batch starts
proposing remedies that survive contact with deployment.

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **AdaFlow-VLA: Adaptive-Step Flow Matching Action Heads with a
  Self-Calibrating Tolerance Schedule** (arXiv:2604.27815, Apr 29, 2026,
  Tsinghua + Shanghai AI Lab, ICLR 2026 under review). Standard
  flow-matching action heads (π₀, FAST-flow, MMaDA-VLA) commit to a
  fixed integration step budget at inference, which over-spends compute
  on easy regimes (free-space reach) and under-spends on hard regimes
  (insertion, contact). AdaFlow-VLA learns a *step-budget head* that
  predicts the local Lipschitz constant of the velocity field and
  dispatches a per-segment integration schedule, paired with a
  **tolerance-aware corridor loss** that is a continuous generalization
  of CorridorVLA (04-26 §1) — small contact deviations are absorbed by
  the tolerance, large drift is penalized. Reports a 1.7× wall-clock
  speedup at fixed LIBERO-Plus / LIBERO-Para success and an absolute
  +4.3 on contact-rich subset. The right structural fix; the
  step-budget head is the missing companion to the corridor loss, and
  the two together are the cleanest *generative-action-head* recipe
  this cycle.

- **MoT-π: Mixture-of-Tokenizers π for Cross-Embodiment Transfer**
  (arXiv:2604.28612, Apr 30, 2026, ByteDance Research). Trains
  embodiment-conditioned tokenizer experts (single-arm, dual-arm,
  bimanual humanoid, mobile-base) inside a π-style backbone, with a
  routing layer that selects the expert from a low-dimensional
  embodiment fingerprint (DoF count, end-effector type, base mobility).
  Avoids the "average tokenizer" failure mode of UniVLA / OpenVLA when
  pretraining mixes embodiments with very different action manifolds.
  Lands in the same design corner as HEX (04-27 §1) and UniT (04-24
  §5) but at the *tokenizer* level rather than the routing or
  reconstruction level — the third independent answer this April that
  embodiment family is the right factoring for cross-embodiment.

- **HiVLA-Lite (Distilled HiVLA into a 1.3B-class deployable head)**
  (arXiv:2604.28104, Apr 28, 2026, carried — distillation companion to
  HiVLA from 04-24). Two-stage knowledge distillation that preserves
  HiVLA's *hierarchical* sub-goal supervision through a token-level
  KL on intermediate plan embeddings, then layer-wise weight transfer
  on the action head. The interesting empirical wrinkle: distilling
  the *plan* layer is what matters; distilling the action layer alone
  loses ~9 points on LIBERO-Long. Useful evidence that hierarchical
  VLAs do not collapse to flat ones when shrunk, *if* you preserve the
  plan-layer supervision.

- *(Carried primaries: ReCAPA (04-27 §1), CorridorVLA (04-26),
  PokéVLA (04-26), HEX (04-27), EmbodiedMidtrain (04-24),
  VLA Foundry (04-24), HiVLA (04-24), HY-Embodied-0.5 (04-24),
  DuoCore-FS (04-22), MMaDA-VLA (04-22), R3D, VGA, A1, SnapFlow,
  StarVLA.)*

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- ★ **GroundFlow: Decoupling Skill Execution from Referent Resolution
  in Open-Vocabulary VLAs** (arXiv:2604.27982, Apr 29, 2026, Stanford
  + Allen Institute). Direct, well-targeted response to *Robust Skills,
  Brittle Grounding* (04-27 §2): the key observation is that VLA
  failures on multi-object commands are dominated by *referent
  resolution* — selecting the right object — rather than skill
  execution. GroundFlow factors the policy into (i) a frozen-VLM
  *referent module* trained with hard-negative contrastive grounding
  and (ii) a skill-conditioned action head that takes a *resolved
  spatial mask* rather than raw language. Cleanly improves the
  language-conditioned task success on Mini-BEHAVIOR-Gran from 41.2 to
  73.0 without changing the skill backbone, which is exactly the
  diagnostic dependency that 04-27 §2 predicted. The architectural
  argument is conservative — a clean factoring rather than a new
  representation — and that is its strength.

- **DiagCoT-VLA: Chain-of-Thought That Surfaces Failure Hypotheses**
  (arXiv:2604.28301, Apr 30, 2026, Microsoft Research). Extends
  DualCoT-VLA (04-22) by training the reasoning trace to *also*
  enumerate plausible failure hypotheses ("the gripper may be too far
  left", "the cup may be occluded") and condition the action on the
  rejected branches. Surprisingly effective on EUEA-style recovery
  tasks (+11.4 success), and the failure-hypothesis branch is a
  natural input layer for KITE (04-27 §2): KITE provides the *what
  failed* layer, DiagCoT-VLA provides the *why might it fail next* prior.

- *(Carried primaries: Robust Skills, Brittle Grounding (04-27),
  PDF (04-26), KITE (04-27), EUEA (04-24), Mini-BEHAVIOR-Gran (04-22),
  ProGAL-VLA (04-22), DualCoT-VLA (04-22), LangForce/Bayesian-VLA
  (04-19), IGAR (04-19), Point-VLA (04-19).)*

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- ★ **MemFlow-VLA: A Retrieval-Augmented Action Head with Episodic
  Memory Distillation** (arXiv:2604.27551, Apr 28, 2026, CUHK + SJTU).
  Most memory-VLAs (MemoryVLA, EchoVLA, MemER) bolt an external memory
  bank onto a frozen action head and read from it via attention, which
  silently leaks distribution shift between train-time and test-time
  retrieval. MemFlow-VLA instead *distills* the episodic memory into a
  small auxiliary parameter set (<3% of policy size) that is updated
  online at episode boundaries, and reads from it through a
  conditional flow head — so the retrieval interface is explicitly
  in-distribution. SOTA on COIN long-horizon, +6.8 on TrackVLA++'s
  long-horizon split, and notably the first memory-VLA whose
  performance does *not* degrade as the memory bank grows past
  ~2k episodes. The right answer to the long-running question of
  whether episodic memory should be *retrieved* or *amortized* —
  this paper says: amortize at episode boundary, retrieve within
  episode.

- **EvoEEAgent v2 (camera-ready update)** (arXiv:2604.13533v3, Apr 30,
  2026). The v3 carries new ablations on the long-term reflector that
  are worth flagging: the long-term loss converges *only* when the
  short-term reflector is conditioned on KITE-style tokenized failure
  evidence rather than raw video. Aligns the EvoEEAgent design with
  this week's GroundFlow + KITE + DiagCoT-VLA stack — the
  reflective-memory branch and the failure-evidence branch are not
  independent.

- *(Carried primaries: ChemBot / Skill-VLA (04-26), EvoEEAgent (04-27),
  COIN (04-24), Goal2Skill (04-22), MemER / MemoryVLA / EchoVLA /
  TrackVLA++ / Critic-in-the-Loop, MEM, Long-VLA, LiLo-VLA.)*

## 4. World-Model-Based VLA

- **PersistDream: Persistent-State Generative World Models with
  Action-Conditioned 3D Memory** (arXiv:2604.28004, Apr 30, 2026,
  Westlake + Tsinghua). Closest follow-up to EgoSim (04-27 §4),
  framed as a generative-video-WM rather than a simulator-WM: keeps a
  persistent latent 3D state grid that is action-conditioned and
  rolled forward across long episodes, cross-attended into a video
  diffusion backbone. Reports the first competitive long-horizon
  rollout result (>200 steps without scene drift) for a generative-
  video-WM, closing what was the most concrete gap between EgoSim and
  DreamZero. With WAV (04-26 §4), Cortex 2.0 (04-24), GigaWorld-Policy
  (04-22), DreamZero (04-22), EgoSim (04-27), and now PersistDream,
  the world-model-VLA taxonomy has its first stable five-cell shape:
  *backbone / planner / data-engine / simulator / persistent-state*.

- **VLA-MBPO++: Stronger Model-Based RL for VLA via Penalized World
  Imagination** (arXiv:2604.28910, May 1, 2026). Updates the
  VLA-MBPO line with a *penalized* imagination roll-out — the world
  model's predicted reward is shrunk in proportion to the model's
  ensemble disagreement, which yields a conservative bound for
  off-policy planning. Stable on contact-rich subset where the
  original VLA-MBPO collapsed, and a useful ablation for the
  world-model-RL discussion that VLAW and VLA-MBPO opened in March.

- *(Carried primaries: EgoSim (04-27), WAV (04-26), Cortex 2.0 (04-24),
  RoboWM-Bench (04-24), MultiWorld (04-24), VAG (04-24), DreamZero
  (04-22), GigaWorld-Policy (04-22), VLA-World (04-19), Veo-Act
  (04-27), Uni-World VLA, VLAW, SC-VLA, World-VLA-Loop, RISE,
  DreamPlan.)*

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **DiscreteFlow-VLA: Quantized Latent Actions with a Continuous
  Decoder Head** (arXiv:2604.28220, Apr 30, 2026, Berkeley + Google
  DeepMind). Argues that the latent-vs-explicit debate (CorridorVLA's
  anchors vs. UniT's tokens, raised in 04-26 §5 and 04-27 §5) has been
  a false dichotomy. DiscreteFlow-VLA learns a *discrete* latent
  action codebook (à la VQ-VLA) at the planner level but pairs it with
  a *continuous* flow-matching decoder, so high-level intent is
  symbolic and low-level execution is smooth. Empirically dominant on
  the LIBERO-Plus contact-rich subset and on cross-embodiment LBM Eval,
  and notably the *only* latent-action method this cycle that does not
  lose performance under sparse-anchor evaluation. The right way out
  of the latent-vs-explicit debate: not pick a side, factor the policy
  so that each level uses what it is good at.

- **JALA-2 (cross-task latent transfer)** (arXiv:2604.28477, Apr 30,
  2026). Extends JALA (04-22) with an explicit *cross-task* latent
  alignment loss that ties together the latent action manifolds of
  related tasks (e.g. all "pour" variants share a low-frequency
  manifold, all "insert" variants another). Modest absolute gains
  (+2.1 LIBERO-X) but qualitatively interesting: the manifolds it
  recovers are interpretable as a primitive vocabulary, which is
  what the latent-action line has been promising since LAPA.

- *(Carried primaries: UniT (04-24), JALA (04-22), LaMP (04-22),
  Mean-Flow One-Step VLA (04-22), StreamingVLA (04-22), LAPA, UniLACT,
  UniVLA, LoLA, LatBot, WholeBodyVLA, Gemini Robotics 1.5 UVMC,
  VLA-JEPA, XL-VLA, VQ-VLA, DIAL.)*

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **AlignRL-VLA: Preference-Based RL for VLA Post-Training Without
  Reward Engineering** (arXiv:2604.27340, Apr 28, 2026, DeepMind +
  ETH Zürich). Most VLA-RL recipes (SimpleVLA-RL, VLA-RL, VLA-RFT,
  VLAJS) require a hand-engineered task-success reward, which scales
  poorly across the BEHAVIOR-1K-class task distribution. AlignRL-VLA
  drops the success reward and replaces it with a *preference* signal
  from a frozen VLM judge, optimized through a stable offline DPO-like
  variant adapted for action distributions (the "action-DPO" loss).
  Matches SimpleVLA-RL's LIBERO-Long 97.6 from preference data alone,
  and crucially scales to 47 unrelated household tasks where reward
  authoring is intractable. The cleanest argument yet that the
  "RLHF for VLAs" framing is more than analogical — and the right
  systems answer to AcceRL (04-24) and VLAJS (04-26): AcceRL gave the
  asynchronous substrate, VLAJS the regularization recipe, AlignRL the
  reward.

- **WoVR-2: World-Model-in-the-Loop Verifier RL** (arXiv:2604.28840,
  Apr 30, 2026). Updates WoVR with a verifier that scores trajectories
  *inside* the world model rather than after rollout, which closes a
  systems gap that was holding world-model-RL back: the verifier no
  longer waits for a full rollout, and PPO can update on shorter
  trajectories. Useful infrastructure paper; pairs with VLA-MBPO++
  (§4 above) on the conservative-imagination branch.

- *(Carried primaries: VLAJS (04-26), AcceRL (04-24), SimpleVLA-RL @
  ICLR 2026 (LIBERO-Long 97.6), FMER (04-22), VLAC (04-22), VLA-RL,
  VLA-RFT, ViVa, TwinRL-VLA, TT-VLA, GigaBrain-0.5M★, IRL-VLA, EvoVLA,
  RTC + Mixture of Horizons, RLinf-VLA, RobustVLA, CRL-VLA, VLA-R1,
  RL-VLA3, WoVR.)*

## 7. Benchmarks, Simulators & Evaluation

- ★ **ProcessBench-Embodied: A Process-Aware Evaluation Suite for
  Long-Horizon VLA** (arXiv:2604.28033, Apr 30, 2026, MIT CSAIL +
  CMU). The direct concrete answer to *How VLAs (Really) Work*
  (04-26 §7). Re-instruments 600 BEHAVIOR-1K-style tasks with
  *process-level* annotations: unsafe interactions, near-misses,
  object damage, recovery-via-luck, partial-success without progress,
  along with an automatic VLM grader for each. Reports the same
  conclusion *How VLAs (Really) Work* did, but quantitatively across
  20 open VLAs: average success rate is 31% lower under process-aware
  scoring than under terminal-state scoring, and the ranking is
  reshuffled — three top-3 systems on the old metric drop out of
  top-10 on the new. Likely to become the de-facto evaluation harness
  for the long-horizon line; the right scaffolding underneath the
  *deployment-credible VLA* push.

- **vla-eval-2.0** (arXiv:2604.28778, Apr 30, 2026; supersedes
  2603.13966). The vla-eval harness adds (a) BEHAVIOR-1K-Process
  hooks, (b) a humanoid sub-suite covering UniT / HTD / SynAgent /
  HEX / WholeBodyVLA, and (c) parallel-rollout cost models so users
  can budget evaluation runs against compute. With ProcessBench-
  Embodied (above) and RoboPlayground (04-27), the queryable
  evaluation surface that 04-27's cross-cutting observation predicted
  is now actually buildable.

- *(Carried primaries: How VLAs (Really) Work (04-26),
  Open-H-Embodiment / GR00T-H (04-26), RoboWM-Bench (04-24),
  COIN (04-24), LBM Eval (04-24), vla-eval (2603.13966),
  RoboPlayground (04-27), StarVLA multi-benchmark co-training (04-22),
  VLA-Risk (04-19), LIBERO-X (04-22), Mini-BEHAVIOR-Gran (04-22),

## 8. Applications, Humanoid & Safety

- **WholeBodyVLA-Touch: Tactile-Augmented Whole-Body Humanoid
  Control** (arXiv:2604.27870, Apr 29, 2026, OpenDriveLab). Direct
  cross-pollination of HTD (04-27 §8) and WholeBodyVLA: extends the
  unified-latent loco-manipulation backbone with a tactile prediction
  head trained jointly on whole-body proprioception. The new wrinkle
  is a *contact-aware locomotion* mode where the gait controller
  conditions on predicted contact distributions across the whole
  body, not just feet — a small change with a surprisingly large
  effect on stability under contact-rich manipulation while standing.
  HTD focused on hand contact; this paper is the natural full-body
  generalization.

- **AVA: Autonomous-Driving VLA Agent with Latent Hazard Memory**
  (arXiv:2604.28250, Apr 30, 2026, Tsinghua + DiDi). UniDriveVLA-style
  driving VLA augmented with a compact *hazard memory*: a small
  rolling buffer of recent dangerous interactions (cut-ins, occluded
  pedestrians, slick-road events) cross-attended into the planning
  head. Reports a meaningful drop in *long-tail* failure rate (~38%
  relative on the rare-event subset of the company's internal eval)
  while leaving common-case driving roughly unchanged. The
  application-side analogue of MemFlow-VLA (§3 above): episodic
  memory as the long-tail patch, not as a general lift.

- **SafetyVLA-Audit (carried-and-sharpened)** (arXiv:2604.26110,
  Apr 27, 2026). Auditing framework for VLA deployments with a
  systematic protocol over four risk axes (instruction misgrounding,
  contact safety, recovery, escalation). Useful complement to
  ProcessBench-Embodied (§7 above): ProcessBench measures *whether*
  the policy is unsafe; SafetyVLA-Audit measures *under what
  perturbations* it becomes unsafe. The two together are now the
  cleanest argument the field has for a deployment-credibility
  evaluation stack.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the post-training stack is settling

The Apr 28 – May 1 batch makes the *post-training* stack for VLAs
feel close to settled. Three independent papers fill three different
slots without overlap:

1. **Reward**: *AlignRL-VLA* (§6) — preference-based action-DPO,
   removes the per-task hand-engineered success reward.
2. **Substrate**: *AcceRL* (04-24 §6) — asynchronous PPO over many
   simulators, removes the throughput bottleneck.
3. **Regularizer**: *VLAJS* (04-26 §6) — annealed VLA prior,
   removes the cold-start exploration bottleneck.

Stack them and you get a recipe — *VLAJS jump-start → AcceRL
substrate → AlignRL preference reward* — that does not require a
hand-engineered reward, does not require a slow synchronous loop, and
does not require the policy to start from random. The strongest
remaining gap is what AlignRL itself flags: a *judging VLM* trained
specifically as a robot-action critic, rather than a frozen general
VLM. Expect that paper within the next two arXiv cycles.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — process-aware evaluation has arrived

If the Apr 23–26 batch raised the alarm on terminal-state scoring
(*How VLAs (Really) Work*), the Apr 28 – May 1 batch operationalizes
the fix:

- **ProcessBench-Embodied** (§7 above): a re-instrumented BEHAVIOR-
  1K with process-level annotations and an automatic VLM grader.
- **vla-eval-2.0** (§7 above): the harness side, with humanoid
  sub-suite and cost-aware parallelism.
- **SafetyVLA-Audit** (§8 above): the perturbation-based stress-test
  side.

This is the first cycle where evaluation has *outpaced* architecture
in the VLA line, and the discontinuity matters: the next ICLR /
NeurIPS submission window is likely to be the first where reviewers
treat process-aware scoring as default. Architecture papers that do
not report on at least one process-aware metric should expect
pushback.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — memory-VLA is consolidating

After two months of fragmentation (MemoryVLA, EchoVLA, MemER,
TrackVLA++, Critic-in-the-Loop, ChemBot/Skill-VLA, EvoEEAgent), the
memory-VLA line is converging on a recognizable interface:

- *Within-episode* working memory, attended at every action step.
- *Across-episode* episodic memory, **amortized into auxiliary
  parameters at episode boundary** (MemFlow-VLA §3 above) rather than
  retrieved as raw trajectories.
- *Across-episode* symbolic memory for protocols and rules (ChemBot,
  EvoEEAgent), updated through reflection loops conditioned on
  failure evidence (KITE / DiagCoT-VLA).

The amortize-at-boundary, retrieve-within-episode design that
MemFlow-VLA articulated this week is, in retrospect, what the
older retrieval-augmented memory-VLAs were converging toward
without naming it. Expect the next wave of memory-VLA papers to
adopt the amortization view explicitly.

## Picks of the day (if you only read three)

1. **AlignRL-VLA (2604.27340)** — preference-based action-DPO that
   removes the hand-engineered reward from VLA-RL, matches
   SimpleVLA-RL on LIBERO-Long, and scales to 47 unrelated tasks.
   Plus the natural completion of the AcceRL + VLAJS post-training
   stack.
2. **MemFlow-VLA (2604.27551)** — the first memory-VLA whose
   performance does not degrade as the memory bank grows.
   Amortize-at-episode-boundary is the design lesson the rest of the
   memory-VLA line is converging toward.
3. **ProcessBench-Embodied (2604.28033)** — the concrete
   benchmark instantiation of *How VLAs (Really) Work*, with
   process-level annotations across 600 long-horizon tasks. Likely
   to become the default long-horizon harness within a quarter, and
   reshuffles the leaderboard exactly where the field needs it
   reshuffled.

Honorable mention: **AdaFlow-VLA (2604.27815)** — adaptive-step
flow matching with a corridor-aware tolerance loss; the cleanest
generative-action-head recipe of the cycle.
