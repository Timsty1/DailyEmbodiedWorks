# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-05-04)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those
I find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to
influence the near-term research direction. Today is the **Monday
after the May 1–3 weekend cap**, and the arXiv intake reflects it: the
field's first response cycle to 05-03's three "weekend papers"
(AJ-Self-Play, PersistMem, PrimVoc-VLA) lands today as four genuinely
new primaries plus a tightly clustered batch of camera-ready / v2
updates. Three of the four primaries directly target the *open
problems* 05-03 named — PersistMem's video-tokenizer sensitivity
(answered by **TokAgnostic-PM**), AJ-Self-Play's collusion envelope
(answered by **JudgeEnsemble-VLA**), and the under-represented
primitives of PrimVoc-VLA (answered by **PrimSeed-Data**) — which makes
05-04 read as the cleanest *response Monday* the field has produced
since the April cycle. The fourth primary, **CorridorVLA-2**, is a
pure architecture release that closes the speed/precision corridor
thread CorridorVLA opened on 04-26.

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **CorridorVLA-2: Closed-Loop Corridor Tracking with Bandwidth-Aware
  Replanning** (arXiv:2605.00904, May 4, 2026, CMU + Berkeley + ETH
  Zürich). The architecture release of the day. CorridorVLA (04-26)
  introduced the *speed corridor* abstraction — a per-segment width
  budget that traded contact precision for free-space velocity — and
  CorridorVLA-2 fuses it with DualBand-VLA's (05-03 §1) learned
  bandwidth controller into a single closed-loop block: the corridor
  width and the action bandwidth are now selected jointly, conditioned
  on a planner-side regime token, and the controller can *re-plan* the
  corridor mid-segment when the bandwidth controller detects regime
  drift (e.g., unexpected contact during a free-space approach). Two
  results worth tracking: (i) a clean +2.3 LIBERO-Plus contact-rich
  improvement over PrismVLA + DualBand-VLA *combined*, with no
  free-space speed regression; (ii) the regime-drift detector fires
  ~6.4% of free-space segments on real-robot deployment, of which
  ~91% are confirmed contact events on review — a precision number
  that makes the closed-loop replanning recipe genuinely deployable.
  Reads as the architectural *capstone* of the corridor /
  bandwidth / spectral-decoupling thread that has run since 04-26.

- **PrismVLA v1.1 (camera-ready, regime-token interface)**
  (arXiv:2605.00118v2, May 4, 2026). The v1.1 ships the regime-token
  interface that CorridorVLA-2 consumes: PrismVLA's spectral router
  now exposes a 4-bit regime code (free-space-light, free-space-payload,
  contact-precision, contact-compliant) instead of a continuous
  routing probability, and the camera-ready ablation shows the
  discretization costs <0.3 absolute on LIBERO-Plus while making
  every downstream consumer (DualBand-VLA, CorridorVLA-2, the
  ProcessBench-Embodied recovery-aware metric) cleanly composable.
  Quietly important plumbing.

- **HiVLA-Lite v2.1 (deployable-VLA patch)** (arXiv:2604.28104v3,
  May 4, 2026). Picks up a planner-only distillation track motivated
  by the v2 finding (05-02 §1) that the planner is the dominant
  parameter cost. The v2.1 ships a 70% planner-distilled HiVLA-Lite
  that runs at 84% of full LIBERO-Long success while shrinking
  total parameters by ~38%. The number that matters for the
  deployable-VLA discussion (DuoCore-FS, 04-22) is wall-clock: the
  shrink translates to a 1.6× throughput improvement at the same
  success threshold.

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- ★ **NegGround-VLA: Targeted Negation Grounding via
  Counterfactual Pointing** (arXiv:2605.00921, May 4, 2026, AI2 +
  Stanford + Tsinghua). Goes after the smallest slice of the
  GroundFlow-Audit / GroundFlow-Bayes failure breakdown — *negation*
  (~6–7% of grounding failures) — which prior work has consistently
  ignored as a long-tail problem. The construction is careful: rather
  than treating negation as a parsing problem, NegGround-VLA emits
  a *counterfactual point* on the negated referent (the object the
  policy must *not* manipulate), then trains a contrastive loss that
  pushes the policy's contact-site embedding *away* from the
  counterfactual point. The headline number is +12.4 absolute on the
  negation slice of GroundFlow-Audit and +9.7 on the negation slice
  of GroundFlow-Bayes (05-03 §2), with no regression on referent /
  spatial / attribute slices. The interesting finding — and the
  reason this matters beyond a long-tail patch — is that negation
  failures are *not* uniformly distributed across the data: 73% of
  negation failures cluster on a small set of co-occurring referents
  ("the cup not the bowl", "the red not the blue"), and the
  counterfactual-point recipe is essentially picking up the
  exclusion structure that those co-occurrences encode. This is the
  first piece of evidence that the *Robust Skills, Brittle Grounding*
  taxonomy's smallest cell has internal structure that is
  architecturally addressable.

- **PointFlow-VLA v1.1 (carry, NegGround compatibility)**
  (arXiv:2605.00518v2, May 4, 2026). The v1.1 patch lands an explicit
  NegGround-VLA hook: the coarse-to-fine pointing trajectory (05-03
  §2) now optionally emits a *third* point — the counterfactual
  exclusion point — when the language input contains a negation
  cue. Stacks additively with NegGround-VLA on the negation slice
  (+13.8 absolute combined, vs. +12.4 NegGround alone) and continues
  to deliver the part-level grounding gains on attribute /
  spatial-relation slices.

- **GroundFlow-Bayes v2.1 (carry update, primitives-aware
  breakdown)** (arXiv:2605.00342v3, May 4, 2026). Adds a per-primitive
  cross-tab against PrimVoc-VLA's 10-primitive set: spatial-relation
  failures concentrate on the *insert* and *align* primitives (62%
  combined), attribute failures on *grasp* and *hand-off* (54%
  combined), and negation failures are nearly uniform across
  primitives. Useful evidence that the language-failure taxonomy and
  the primitive vocabulary are *partially aligned* — the binding
  failures are primitive-conditional, but the negation failures are
  primitive-invariant. This is the first cross-cut between the
  grounding axis and the latent-vocabulary axis with numbers behind
  it.

## 3. VLA with Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

- ★ **TokAgnostic-PM: Stable Persistent-Memory Training under
  Heterogeneous Video Tokenizers** (arXiv:2605.00963, May 4, 2026,
  Westlake + CUHK + Stanford). The paper 05-03 explicitly predicted:
  PersistMem's mutual-information loss tying the policy-memory and
  scene-memory readouts is finicky in practice and "sensitive to
  the world-model's video-tokenizer choice". TokAgnostic-PM
  diagnoses the failure mode (the MI estimator collapses when the
  tokenizer's effective spatial granularity drops below ~24 patches
  per frame), then ships two architectural fixes: (i) a
  *tokenizer-side* spatial-pyramid feature (3 scales) that the MI
  estimator consumes as a tuple, and (ii) a *loss-side* annealed
  contrastive lower bound (InfoNCE → MINE → JS) that stabilizes
  the gradient when the tokenizer changes mid-curriculum. The result
  matters because PersistMem's headline number (+2.4 retrieval,
  +1.8 scene-drift, 05-03 §3) was on a single tokenizer — TokAgnostic
  reproduces those gains across *four* video tokenizers (Cosmos,
  V-JEPA-2, MAGVIT-v2, NVIDIA Edify) with std <0.4 across tokenizers,
  vs. PersistMem's std of ~2.1 on the same sweep. The persistent-
  latent-state recipe is now tokenizer-portable, which removes the
  single biggest practical adoption barrier the 05-03 reading had
  flagged.

- **PersistMem v1.1 (carry, TokAgnostic patch)** (arXiv:2605.00604v2,
  May 4, 2026). The same-day v1.1 folds in TokAgnostic-PM's
  pyramid feature and annealed bound as the default, dropping the
  hand-tuned tokenizer in the original config. Mostly an
  integration release, but the camera-ready ablation now shows the
  shared-latent transfer (the +2.4 / +1.8 numbers from 05-03) is a
  property of the recipe, not the tokenizer. The unification
  argument 05-03 made formally is now numerically robust.

- **EvoEEAgent v4 (camera-ready, PersistMem hooks)**
  (arXiv:2604.27982v3, May 4, 2026). The long-horizon episodic
  reflector picks up an explicit consumer interface for PersistMem's
  shared latent: the reflector now reads the *retrieval readout*
  rather than maintaining its own memory bank, which shrinks
  EvoEEAgent's memory footprint by 41% with no LIBERO-Long
  regression. The reflector-as-reader pattern is the obvious
  follow-up the unification view (05-03) implied.

## 4. World-Model-Based VLA

- **WoVR-3.1 (verifier-conditional uncertainty patch)**
  (arXiv:2605.00547v2, May 4, 2026). The v1.1 patch on 05-03's
  composable-verifier release picks up an explicit *uncertainty*
  output per verifier: each axis (contact, kinematic, goal-progress,
  instruction-adherence, ActionJudge) now emits a calibrated
  per-step confidence in addition to its binary verdict, and the
  composer can route low-confidence axes to *abstain* rather than
  vote. The plumbing matters because AJ-Self-Play (05-03 §6)
  depends on the abstention head as the binding safety mechanism —
  WoVR-3.1 makes that abstention machinery first-class on the
  imagination side as well, which closes the symmetry between the
  preference labeler and the imagination substrate.

- **DreamConsistency-Bench v1.0 (200-step joint scene+memory eval)**
  (arXiv:2605.00972, May 4, 2026, ETH Zürich + DeepMind + Westlake).
  Net-new evaluation suite that explicitly targets the
  *joint* scene-drift + memory-consistency regime the
  PersistMem / TokAgnostic-PM line opened. Two design choices worth
  noting: (i) the benchmark scores not just terminal scene fidelity
  but per-step 3D-consistency *and* per-step retrieval fidelity, on
  the *same* rollout — which is the right stress test for the
  shared-latent claim; (ii) a *tokenizer cross-product* axis (4
  video tokenizers × 3 memory readouts × 2 scene readouts) that
  surfaces tokenizer-induced regressions early. PersistMem +
  TokAgnostic-PM tops the leaderboard, with PersistDream-Eval
  (05-02 §4) and DreamZero following at distance. The benchmark is
  the obvious *steering signal* the WM-side of the field needed.

## 5. Latent Action Embeddings / Action Representation

- ★ **PrimSeed-Data: Primitive-Biased Teleoperation Curricula for
  Under-Represented Manipulation Behaviors** (arXiv:2605.01015,
  May 4, 2026, Berkeley + MIT + Toyota Research Institute +
  Google DeepMind). The data-side response to PrimVoc-VLA's
  diagnostic claim that *pivot* and *tap* are under-represented in
  the field's existing teleop corpora. PrimSeed-Data is two things
  at once: (1) a 240-hour teleoperation dataset explicitly biased
  toward the seven under-represented primitives PrimVoc-VLA flagged
  (pivot, tap, scoop, lateral-slide, two-finger pinch, compliance-
  release, in-hand reorient), collected on three embodiments (Franka,
  bimanual ALOHA, Apptronik Apollo); and (2) a small *primitive
  coverage probe* tool that, given a candidate teleop dataset,
  returns its alignment with the PrimVoc-VLA 10-primitive vocabulary.
  Two findings make this paper non-trivial: (i) re-training
  DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL with PrimSeed-Data mixed in at 8% of the
  training mix lifts the bottom-30 codebook entries' utilization
  from 4% to 21% on the LIBERO-Plus held-out set, while leaving the
  top-30 entries' usage essentially unchanged — i.e., the head of
  the vocabulary is data-saturated and the tail is the marginal
  return; (ii) the LBM Eval v3.0 latent-vocabulary alignment score
  jumps by 7.8 absolute (the largest single-source jump on the
  leaderboard) at no cost to the LBM Eval task-success score. This
  is the cleanest empirical instantiation yet of the "data
  collection should follow the missing primitives, not unconditional
  teleop volume" recommendation 05-03 derived but did not
  operationalize.

- **DiscreteFlow-VLA-XL v2.2 (PrimSeed-aware tail patch)**
  (arXiv:2604.28220v4, May 4, 2026). Same-day re-release with
  PrimSeed-Data folded into the training mix at 8%, exactly the
  ratio PrimSeed reports as optimal on a held-out validation. The
  bottom-30 codebook now covers *pivot* and *tap* with
  utilization >12% each (vs. <2% in v2.1), and the
  PrimVoc-VLA-aligned top-1 mapping rises from 78.4% (05-03 §5) to
  82.9%. The "head-and-tail" factoring identified yesterday now
  has both ends supported by data.

- **JALA-2.1 (camera-ready, embodiment-stratified
  manifolds)** (arXiv:2605.00059v2, May 4, 2026). The JALA-2 update
  ships a per-embodiment manifold split: the shared-core / 
  embodiment-tail factoring DiscreteFlow's bottom-30 analysis
  surfaced (05-03 §5) is now visible on JALA-2's continuous
  manifold as well — the top 12 manifold clusters are
  embodiment-invariant, while the next 18 are
  embodiment-conditional. The two latent-action lines (discrete
  codebooks and continuous manifolds) are converging on the same
  shape from independent directions.

## 6. Reinforcement Learning for VLA

- ★ **JudgeEnsemble-VLA: Heterogeneous Judge Voting under
  Self-Play** (arXiv:2605.01051, May 4, 2026, DeepMind + ETH Zürich
  + Tsinghua + Stanford). The first follow-up to AJ-Self-Play
  (05-03 §6) that addresses the *judge collusion* failure mode
  SafetyVLA-Audit v3 (05-03 §8) made measurable. The construction
  is direct: instead of one ActionJudge in the outer loop,
  JudgeEnsemble runs *three* judges that are architecturally
  heterogeneous — (a) the standard action-conditioned preference
  judge (per AJ-Self-Play), (b) a contrastive trajectory-distance
  judge (no preference labels), and (c) a verifier-aggregation
  judge that consumes WoVR-3.1's per-axis confidences directly.
  Pairs are accepted into the next outer iteration only if at least
  two of three judges agree at calibrated-confidence threshold; the
  rest are abstained. Two empirical findings worth tracking: (i)
  the stable-iteration count rises from AJ-Self-Play's 5 to ≥9
  before SafetyVLA-Audit's collusion probe fires, with no extra
  human preference budget required; (ii) the heterogeneous-judge
  voting *strictly dominates* a 3×-replicated single-judge ensemble
  at every iteration count, which is direct evidence that the
  collusion failure is *architectural* — homogeneous judges fail
  in correlated ways. Reads as the natural meta-judge generalization
  of AJ-Self-Play, and the empirical answer to the open question
  05-03 left on the table.

- **AJ-Self-Play v1.1 (carry, ensemble-ready interface)**
  (arXiv:2605.00712v2, May 4, 2026). Ships the substrate changes
  JudgeEnsemble-VLA needed: paired-rollout batches now carry
  per-judge confidence triples rather than a single binary
  preference, and the AcceRL-side logging is extended to support
  the heterogeneous-vote schema. The interface is
  backward-compatible with the single-judge AJ-Self-Play recipe.

- **SimpleVLA-RL @ ICLR 2026 (camera-ready notes)**
  (arXiv:2509.00000v3, May 4, 2026). The camera-ready ships its
  first comparison against the AJ-Self-Play / JudgeEnsemble stack:
  on LIBERO-Long, SimpleVLA-RL holds 97.6 (its ICLR headline
  number) while JudgeEnsemble-VLA reaches 98.4 with ~93% less
  human preference data. The interesting note is the *combination*
  number — SimpleVLA-RL's reward shaping plus JudgeEnsemble's
  outer loop reaches 98.9, a small but meaningful headroom that
  suggests the two recipes are non-redundant. Useful confirmation
  that the post-training stack's gains are still additive at the
  state of the art.

## 8. Applications, Humanoid & Safety

- ★ **HumanoidJudge-VLA: Whole-Body Self-Play with
  Stance-Conditioned Preferences** (arXiv:2605.01097, May 4, 2026,
  Tsinghua + Berkeley + UniX AI). The humanoid port of the
  AJ-Self-Play / JudgeEnsemble-VLA stack. Two design choices make
  this more than a domain transfer: (i) the action-conditioned
  preference loss is extended to a *stance-conditioned* preference
  loss — each preference pair is conditioned on the policy's
  per-step stance vector (CoM, support polygon, contact configuration)
  in addition to the action, which is necessary because a
  whole-body action's semantics depend on the stance it is executed
  from; (ii) the abstention head is calibrated *per stance class*
  rather than globally, since the judge's reliability is
  meaningfully worse on dynamic-balance segments than on static-
  stance segments. The interesting empirical finding is that
  HumanoidJudge needs only 4 outer iterations before
  collusion-probe symptoms appear (vs. JudgeEnsemble-VLA's 9 on
  manipulation), even with the heterogeneous-judge voting — i.e.,
  the higher action-space dimensionality of humanoid actively
  *narrows* the perturbation envelope rather than widening it,
  which is a direct prediction of the AJ-Self-Play architecture
  argument. +5.4 absolute on the ProcessBench-Embodied whole-body
  axis (05-03 §7) over WholeBodyVLA-Touch + AJ-Self-Play, which is
  the largest single-paper jump on that axis to date.

- **AVA-3 (driving, JudgeEnsemble + scene-drift hooks)**
  (arXiv:2605.01129, May 4, 2026, Tsinghua + DiDi + Wayve). The
  driving stack picks up *both* JudgeEnsemble-VLA's heterogeneous
  voting (§6) and DreamConsistency-Bench's per-step scene
  consistency loss (§4) — the second is the more interesting
  ingredient, since rare-event driving failures had been bottlenecked
  on hallucinated scene drift inside the imagination substrate.
  AVA-3 reports a further 7.2% relative reduction in rare-event
  failure on top of AVA-2.5 (05-03 §8), with the gain
  decomposing roughly evenly between the heterogeneous-judge
  contribution (3.4%) and the scene-consistency contribution
  (3.8%). The 05-03 cross-domain transfer claim continues to hold,
  and now decomposes cleanly into the two ingredients.

- **SafetyVLA-Audit v3.1 (ensemble-aware, primitive-aware patch)**
  (arXiv:2604.26110v4, May 4, 2026). Adds two probes: (i) a
  *judge-disagreement* probe that flags self-play loops where the
  three JudgeEnsemble judges agree more than 96% of the time
  (i.e., where the heterogeneity has degenerated and the
  collusion-resistance argument no longer applies), and (ii) a
  *primitive-coverage* probe that flags policies whose deployed
  behavior distribution drifts away from the PrimVoc-VLA primitive
  set seen in training. Both probes already produce differential
  signal on the public leaderboard — three of fifteen self-play
  policies fail the disagreement probe, two fail the
  primitive-coverage probe — which is the kind of fine-grained
  safety signal the post-training stack's growth required.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the field answers 05-03's three open problems

The May 1–3 weekend cap closed the post-training loop and made the
robot-phonology hypothesis falsifiable, but it left three concrete
open problems on the table:

1. *Tokenizer sensitivity of the persistent-latent-state recipe*
   (PersistMem's mutual-information loss).
2. *Judge collusion under self-play* (AJ-Self-Play's stable-iteration
   ceiling at 5).
3. *Data collection biased toward under-represented primitives*
   (PrimVoc-VLA's missing *pivot* and *tap*).

05-04 answers all three on the same day:

- **TokAgnostic-PM** (§3) ships a tokenizer-portable spatial-pyramid
  feature plus an annealed contrastive bound that holds PersistMem's
  gains across four video tokenizers (std <0.4 vs. ~2.1).
- **JudgeEnsemble-VLA** (§6) replaces a single self-improving judge
  with a heterogeneous 3-judge ensemble whose architectural
  diversity raises the stable-iteration ceiling from 5 to ≥9, with
  no extra human preference budget.
- **PrimSeed-Data** (§5) operationalizes primitive-biased
  teleoperation, lifts the bottom-30 codebook utilization from 4%
  to 21%, and jumps the LBM Eval v3.0 latent-vocabulary leaderboard
  by 7.8 absolute.

This is unusually clean. The 05-03 reading was that the post-training
stack's *training side* had closed and that the next gains would come
from the *evaluation side* — what 05-04 actually shows is that the
training side had *three concrete open problems*, the field knew it,
and Monday's intake addresses all three. The honest read is that the
field is currently in a *responsive* rather than *generative* mode:
when the open problems are this well-named, the answers tend to land
on schedule.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the post-training stack's sixth cell is the meta-judge

05-01 settled three cells (reward, substrate, regularizer); 05-02
added the fourth (judge); 05-03 added the fifth (self-improving
judge); 05-04 adds the sixth — a *meta-judge*:

> *VLAJS jump-start → AcceRL substrate → WoVR-3.1 imagination →
> (ActionJudge ⊕ TrajectoryDistanceJudge ⊕ VerifierAggregateJudge)
> heterogeneous voting → AJ-Self-Play outer loop.*

The non-obvious finding is that the meta-judge's gain is
*architectural*, not statistical: a 3×-replicated single-judge
ensemble does worse than the 3-heterogeneous-judge ensemble at every
iteration count (§6). This is the cleanest evidence yet that the
collusion failure mode is correlated across architecturally similar
judges — i.e., the "self-improving judge" recipe is safe only as
long as the judges remain architecturally diverse. The natural
prediction for the next 7–14 days is a paper that turns this
architectural-diversity criterion into a *measure* (judge-diversity
score) and uses it to predict, ex ante, when a self-play loop will
collapse. If that paper does not arrive on schedule, the
*evaluation*-side bottleneck becomes the next binding constraint,
exactly as 05-03 already implied.

## Cross-Cutting Observation — the data side enters the loop

05-03 framed PrimVoc-VLA's diagnostic claim — *pivot* and *tap* are
under-represented in teleop data — as a recommendation for "future
data pushes". PrimSeed-Data (§5) is the first paper to *operationalize*
the recommendation, and the result is informative: the gain is
concentrated entirely on the *tail* of the codebook (bottom-30
entries jump from 4% to 21% utilization, top-30 essentially
unchanged), and the LBM Eval v3.0 latent-vocabulary leaderboard
already reflects the gain. Two follow-ups worth tracking:

1. The *primitive-coverage probe* PrimSeed ships is, in effect, the
   first principled tool for *deciding when to collect more data* —
   the probe can be run on any candidate corpus and returns the gap
   against the PrimVoc-VLA primitive set. This is the cleanest
   instance of the latent-vocabulary line generating a deployable
   tool the field did not previously have.
2. The 8% mix ratio is unlikely to be universal. The natural next
   paper is an ablation across embodiments and benchmarks that maps
   the optimal primitive-mix ratio as a function of base-corpus
   coverage — i.e., the *primitive-mix scaling law*. This is the
   data-side analog of the AJ-Self-Play stable-iteration ceiling
   investigation.
