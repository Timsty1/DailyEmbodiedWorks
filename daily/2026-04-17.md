# Daily Embodied Works — VLA Edition (2026-04-17)

A curated digest of recently released / updated embodied works on
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. Highlighted entries (★) are those I
find particularly well-designed, well-motivated, or likely to influence
the research direction.

---

## 1. VLA Model Design & Architecture

- ★ **StarVLA: A Lego-like Codebase for VLA Model Developing**
  (arXiv:2604.05014, Apr 6, 2026). Modular *backbone–action-head*
  decomposition that unifies VLM backbones (Qwen-VL, PaliGemma) *and*
  world-model backbones (Cosmos) alongside the main action-decoding
  paradigms (autoregressive discrete, continuous regression, flow-matching,
  diffusion). Ships reproducible recipes matching or surpassing prior
  methods on LIBERO / SimplerEnv / RoboTwin 2.0 / RoboCasa-GR1 /
  BEHAVIOR-1K. Likely to become the default reference codebase — the
  community has been badly fragmented around OpenVLA forks.

- **VLANeXt: Recipes for Building Strong VLA Models**
  (arXiv:2602.18532). Distills design choices (vision tokenizer, action
  head coupling, data mixing ratios, LR schedules) into concrete recipes
  with controlled ablations.

- **E-VLA: Event-augmented VLA for Dark and Blurred Scenes**
  (arXiv:2604.04834, Apr 6, 2026). Adds an event-camera stream to a VLA
  so manipulation remains stable under motion blur and low light — a
  principled fix for a real deployment failure mode rather than a new
  benchmark chaser.

- **UAV-Track VLA** (arXiv:2604.02241). Extends VLA-style action
  prediction to embodied aerial tracking; a reminder that the VLA
  interface is useful well beyond tabletop manipulation.

- Industry systems to watch: **Helix** (Figure AI, dual-system
  VLM+fast visuomotor policy for humanoids), **π0 / π0-FAST**
  (Physical Intelligence; FAST introduces DCT-based Frequency-space
  Action Sequence Tokenization), **Gemini Robotics 1.5** (Google
  DeepMind; introduces UVMC — Unified Vision-Motion Codes via
  dual-branch VQ-VAE with a shared codebook).

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

- ★ **ProGAL-VLA: Grounded Alignment through Prospective Reasoning**
  (arXiv:2604.09824, Apr 10, 2026). Separates slow symbolic planning
  from fast control with an explicit verification bottleneck: a 3D
  entity-centric graph + slow planner emits symbolic sub-goals; a
  Grounding Alignment Contrastive loss ties them to grounded entities.
  On LIBERO-Plus, robustness under perturbations jumps **30.3 → 71.5%**
  and *language ignorance* drops 3–4×. Rare example of a VLA paper where
  the mechanism maps cleanly onto the failure mode it cures.

- **IGAR: Restoring Linguistic Grounding via Train-Free Attention
  Recalibration** (arXiv:2603.06001). Diagnoses "linguistic blindness"
  in VLAs (policy obeys visual priors, ignores the instruction) and fixes
  it at inference time by reweighting attention — no retraining.

- **Uncovering Linguistic Fragility via Diversity-Aware Red Teaming**
  (arXiv:2604.05595, Apr 7, 2026). The DAERT framework discovers
  paraphrases that crash π₀ and OpenVLA from **93.33% → 5.85%** success.
  Blunt evidence that instruction-robustness is nowhere near solved.

- **LIBERO-Para** (arXiv:2603.28301). A diagnostic benchmark that adds
  paraphrase perturbations to LIBERO; complements the red-teaming work.

- **ST4VLA**. Argues spatial reasoning is the unifying substrate for
  instruction following, long-horizon manipulation, and multimodal
  grounding.

## 3. VLA with Memory

- ★ **MEM: Multi-Scale Embodied Memory for VLAs** (Physical Intelligence,
  arXiv:2603.03596). Two-scale memory: an efficient video encoder for
  short-horizon frame history, and a language-based store for
  long-horizon context. Gives Gemma-3-4B-class VLAs a **~15-minute
  context** window, enabling tasks like full-kitchen cleanup without
  blowing the runtime budget. The short/long split is the right
  abstraction — raw video context does not scale, and pure language
  summaries lose motor-relevant detail.

- **MemER: Scaling Up Memory via Experience Retrieval** (Stanford,
  arXiv:2510.20328). Hierarchical VLA where a high-level memory policy
  streams observations, nominates keyframes to retain, and emits
  language subtasks for a low-level controller — effectively
  retrieval-augmented robot control. SOTA on three real-world long-horizon
  tasks requiring minutes of memory.

- **MemoryVLA: Perceptual-Cognitive Dual Memory** (arXiv:2508.19236).
  Separate perceptual and cognitive memory banks fused at decision time.

## 4. Benchmarks & Simulators

- ★ **vla-eval: A Unified Evaluation Harness for VLA Models** (AI2,
  arXiv:2603.13966). Model ↔ benchmark decoupling via WebSocket +
  msgpack with Docker-based env isolation. Supports 13 sim benchmarks and
  6 model servers; episode sharding + batched GPU inference gives
  **47× throughput** (2,000 LIBERO episodes in ~18 min on one H100). The
  accompanying leaderboard aggregates 657 published results across 17
  benchmarks. This is exactly the infra the field has been missing.

- **SimX-OR**. Drops into any existing sim benchmark and systematically
  stress-tests VLAs along observational robustness axes (camera pose,
  lighting, distractors, textures).

- **VLABench** (ICCV 2025, still a primary reference). Large-scale
  language-conditioned manipulation with long-horizon compositional
  tasks.

- **LIBERO-Plus / LIBERO-Para**. Perturbation- and paraphrase-augmented
  splits used by several of the robustness papers above.

## 5. VLA Reinforcement Learning

- ★ **SimpleVLA-RL** (ICLR 2026, github: PRIME-RL/SimpleVLA-RL). A
  minimalist but effective RL post-training recipe for VLAs. Under data
  scarcity it beats SFT by a wide margin in both sim and real, and —
  notably — *improves* spatial / object / goal generalization rather
  than overfitting. Strong argument that RL is finally tractable for
  full-scale VLA policies.

- ★ **VLA-RFT: RL Fine-Tuning with Verified Rewards in World
  Simulators** (arXiv:2510.00406). Uses a learned world model as a
  controllable simulator and derives dense trajectory-level rewards from
  goal-achieving references. Exceeds strong SFT baselines in **<400
  fine-tuning steps**. The cleanest answer yet to "where does the reward
  signal come from?" for VLAs.

- **RLinf-VLA** (arXiv:2510.06710). Unified RL-for-VLA framework
  supporting heterogeneous simulators, multiple RL algorithms, and a
  flexible resource-allocation layer (1.61–1.88× training speedup).

- **VLA-RL** (arXiv:2505.18719). Trajectory-level RL formulated as
  multi-modal multi-turn conversation over a pretrained autoregressive
  VLA.

- **ViVa: Video-Generative Value Model for Robot RL**
  (arXiv:2604.08168, Apr 9, 2026). Repurposes a pretrained video
  generator as a value function (predicts future proprio + scalar
  value). Reports **73% success** at **14 successful tasks/hour**. An
  underrated idea — value functions have been the weakest link in real-
  robot RL.

- **IRL-VLA** (arXiv:2508.06571). Close-loop RL via inverse RL with a
  lightweight learned reward world model (autonomous driving).

- **EvoVLA: Self-Evolving VLA** (arXiv:2511.16166). Self-improvement
  loop without external supervision.

## 6. World-Model-Based VLA

- ★ **Chain-of-World (CoWVLA)** (arXiv:2603.03195, Mar 3, 2026). Unifies
  world modeling and latent-action learning into a single continuous
  latent-motion chain with terminal keyframe prediction. Keeps the
  interpretability/compactness of latent motion while inheriting the
  temporal reasoning of video world models. A rare architecture where
  the two traditionally-separate pipelines collapse into one.

- ★ **DIAL: Decoupling Intent and Action via Latent World Modeling**
  (arXiv:2603.29844). A VLM-based System-2 performs latent world
  modeling by synthesizing *latent visual foresight* in the vision
  encoder's native feature space; a differentiable intent bottleneck
  feeds the motor controller. End-to-end, but the bottleneck is
  inspectable.

- **VLA-JEPA: Enhancing VLA with a Latent World Model**
  (arXiv:2602.10098). JEPA-style predictive objective applied in VLA
  space.

- **World-VLA-Loop** (arXiv:2602.06508). Closed-loop co-training of a
  video world model and a VLA policy.

- **VideoVLA: Video Generators Can Be Generalizable Robot
  Manipulators**. Multimodal DiT that jointly predicts the action
  sequence *and* the future visual outcome.

- **1X World Model (1XWM)**. Policy derived from a text-conditioned
  video generator — an industry take on the same bet.

- **OpenWorldLib** (arXiv:2604.04707). Unified inference framework over
  world-model tasks (interactive video gen, 3D gen, multimodal
  reasoning, VLA).

## 7. Latent Action Embeddings

- ★ **JALA: Joint-Aligned Latent Action — Scalable VLA Pretraining in
  the Wild** (arXiv:2602.21736). Bypasses LAPA's multi-stage
  reconstruction pipeline and learns a predictive action embedding
  jointly aligned with inverse dynamics *and* real robot actions. The
  variant **JALA-dino hits 96.9% avg success** with **no pretraining on
  robotic data**. Motivated cleanly: reconstruction pseudo-labels waste
  capacity modeling visual appearance you don't need at control time.

- **LAPA (ICLR 2025, still the baseline)**. VQ-VAE latent actions over
  image pairs + two-stage finetune; 30× pretraining efficiency vs.
  action-labeled pretraining.

- **LAWM: Latent Action Pretraining Through World Modeling**
  (arXiv:2509.18428). Model-agnostic, self-supervised latent action
  pretraining from unlabeled video.

- **UniLACT: Depth-Aware RGB Latent Action Learning** (arXiv:2602.20231).
  Produces modality-specific and unified latent-action representations
  serving as pseudo-labels.

- **UniVLA (ICLR 2026)** (arXiv:2506.19850). Unified autoregressive
  token sequence over vision/language/action; world-modeling
  post-training step that captures causal dynamics from video. SOTA on
  CALVIN, LIBERO, SimplerEnv-Bridge.

- **Gemini Robotics 1.5 — UVMC**. Industry counterpart: discrete latent
  jointly encoding visual dynamics and robot motion via dual-branch
  VQ-VAE with shared codebook.

## 8. Dual-System / Fast-Slow Architectures (cross-cutting theme)

- **Fast-in-Slow (FiS-VLA)** (arXiv:2506.01953). Embeds the System-1
  executor inside the System-2 VLM by partial parameter sharing.
  Reports +8% sim, +11% real, and **117.7 Hz** control.

- **DuoCore-FS** (Astribot, arXiv:2512.20188). Commercial-scale
  fast/slow split with an explicit async boundary, targeting the VLM
  inference-latency bottleneck for real-time humanoid control.

- Relation to the rest of the field: ProGAL-VLA, DIAL, and Helix are
  all fundamentally dual-system — this appears to be the converging
  architectural consensus for 2026.

## Meta: what the ICLR 2026 cohort says about the field

Moritz Reuss' analysis of 164 VLA submissions to ICLR 2026 surfaces
three clear trends: (i) **discrete-diffusion VLAs** combining tokenizer
simplicity with diffusion's trajectory modeling; (ii) an influx of
**reasoning-augmented** VLAs; (iii) continued dominance of
**LIBERO / CALVIN / SIMPLER** as shared evaluation substrate — which is
why vla-eval and SimX-OR matter. RL post-training has moved from
"niche" to "expected", and world-model-based approaches are colonizing
both pretraining (latent actions) and post-training (reward/value).

## Picks of the day (if you only read three)

1. **ProGAL-VLA** — the clearest cure for linguistic blindness so far.
2. **VLA-RFT** + **SimpleVLA-RL** — RL for VLAs is now actually working.
3. **Chain-of-World (CoWVLA)** — latent actions and world models finally
   collapse into one model instead of two stacked pipelines.
