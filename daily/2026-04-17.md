# Daily Embodied Works — 2026-04-17

A digest of recent Vision-Language-Action (VLA) and embodied-AI work, biased
toward papers released in the first half of April 2026 (arXiv `2604.*`).
Curator's highlights are flagged **[Highlight]** with a short rationale.

---

## 1. VLA Model Design

### **[Highlight]** StarVLA: A Lego-like Codebase for VLA Development
- arXiv: [2604.05014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05014) · Code:
  [github.com/starVLA/starVLA](https://github.com/starVLA/starVLA)
- TL;DR: Proposes a *backbone–action-head* decomposition in which a shared
  vision-language backbone produces scene+instruction embeddings that feed a
  pluggable action head (AR token, diffusion, flow-matching, world-model).
  Unifies LIBERO / SimplerEnv / RoboTwin 2.0 / RoboCasa-GR1 / BEHAVIOR-1K
  under one evaluation interface and ships reproducible single-benchmark
  training recipes that already match or beat prior methods.
- Why it matters: VLA research has been painfully fragmented across
  incompatible codebases. A modular, well-benchmarked framework that cleanly
  separates "policy head design" from "backbone pretraining" is exactly the
  piece the community needs for principled ablation — arguably the most
  *infrastructurally* important VLA paper of the month.

### StarVLA-α: Reducing Complexity in VLA Systems
- arXiv: [2604.11757](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.11757v1)
- Companion study to StarVLA that deliberately *minimizes* architectural
  variation (Qwen3-VL backbone, no robot-specific pretraining) to isolate
  the contribution of action-modeling choices, robot pretraining, and
  interface engineering. A useful baseline against a field that has drifted
  toward baroque designs.

### SnapFlow: One-Step Action Generation for Flow-Matching VLAs
- arXiv: [2604.05656](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05656)
- Self-distillation that collapses 10-step flow-matching denoising into a
  single NFE by mixing standard flow-matching loss with consistency samples
  whose targets are *two-step Euler shortcut velocities* computed from the
  model's own marginal predictions. A learnable target-time projection lets
  the network differentiate the two objectives.
- Results on π0.5 (3B) across 4 LIBERO suites: **98.75%** average success
  (slightly above the 10-step teacher), 9.6× denoising speedup, end-to-end
  latency 274 ms → 83 ms, trained in ~12 h on one GPU. No external teacher.

### **[Highlight]** HY-Embodied-0.5 (Tencent Hunyuan)
- arXiv: [2604.07430](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07430) · Code:
  [github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HY-Embodied](https://github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HY-Embodied)
- Family of embodied foundation models (2B for edge, 32B for reasoning)
  built on a **Mixture-of-Transformers (MoT)** backbone with latent tokens
  for fine-grained perception. Uses on-policy distillation from the 32B to
  the 2B variant. The 32B is competitive with Gemini 3.0 Pro on embodied
  understanding, and the VLM is used as the backbone of a downstream VLA.
- Why it matters: First well-scoped attempt at a *dedicated* embodied
  foundation-model stack (not a repurposed general VLM), with a clear
  teacher-student deployment story for edge robots.

### Open-Loop Planning, Closed-Loop Verification (SV-VLA)
- arXiv: [2604.02965](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02965)
- Speculative-decoding-style split: a heavy VLA low-frequency macro-planner
  emits action chunks plus a planning context; a lightweight verifier
  continuously compares the planned action against a closed-loop reference
  and only triggers replanning when needed. Clean way to get the efficiency
  of chunking without its open-loop fragility.

### E-VLA: Event-Augmented VLA for Dark / Blurred Scenes
- arXiv: [2604.04834](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04834)
- Adds an event-camera stream to a VLA for robustness when RGB fails
  (low-light, motion blur). A small but important reminder that VLA sensing
  is still RGB-heavy.

## 2. Instruction Following & Language Grounding

### **[Highlight]** ProGAL-VLA: Grounded Alignment through Prospective Reasoning
- arXiv: [2604.09824](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09824)
- Attacks the well-documented "language ignorance" failure mode of VLAs
  (policies lean on visual shortcuts and ignore instruction edits). Builds
  a 3D entity-centric graph, uses a slow planner to produce symbolic
  sub-goals, and aligns them with grounded entities via a
  **Grounding-Alignment Contrastive (GAC)** loss. All actions are
  conditioned on a verified goal embedding whose attention entropy doubles
  as an ambiguity signal.
- Results on LIBERO-Plus: robustness under robot perturbation 30.3 → **71.5%**,
  language ignorance reduced 3-4×, entity retrieval R@1 0.41 → 0.71.
  On a custom ambiguity benchmark: AUROC 0.81 (vs. 0.52) and clarification
  rate on ambiguous inputs 0.09 → 0.81 without harming unambiguous success.
- Why it matters: "Does the robot actually listen?" is an increasingly
  important axis for VLA evaluation, and this paper addresses it with a
  principled decoupling of grounding and execution plus a useful
  intrinsic-ambiguity signal.

### Uncovering Linguistic Fragility via Diversity-Aware Red Teaming
- arXiv: [2604.05595](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05595)
- Adversarial instruction search against π0 and OpenVLA. Average task
  success crashes **93.33% → 5.85%** under rephrased but semantically
  identical instructions. A sobering benchmark for instruction robustness.

## 3. Memory & Long-Horizon Reasoning

### **[Highlight]** MemoryVLA: Perceptual-Cognitive Memory
- arXiv: [2508.19236](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.19236)
- Cognition-Memory-Action framework: a pretrained VLM encodes each
  observation into perceptual + cognitive tokens that populate a
  two-tier **Perceptual-Cognitive Memory Bank** (low-level details +
  high-level semantics). On 12 real-world tasks MemoryVLA reaches
  **84.0%** success, with long-horizon tasks showing **+26 pts** over
  SOTA. A clean, interpretable memory design that treats perception and
  cognition asymmetrically — likely a template for future work.

### LoLA: Long-Horizon Latent Action Learning
- arXiv: [2512.20166](https://arxiv.org/html/2512.20166)
- Conditions on historical frames + multi-view images + language to handle
  long-horizon manipulation across different embodiments via a shared
  latent-action space.

### TrackVLA++: Reasoning and Memory for Embodied Tracking
- arXiv: [2510.07134](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.07134)
- Adds explicit reasoning and memory modules to a tracking-oriented VLA;
  useful reference for memory design in pursuit/navigation settings.

### Keyframe-Chaining VLA
- arXiv: [2603.01465](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.01465)
- Models non-Markovian long-horizon manipulation by chaining sparse
  semantic keyframes instead of using full long-context attention.

## 4. World-Model-based VLA

### **[Highlight]** VLA-JEPA: VLA with a Latent World Model
- arXiv: [2602.10098](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.10098)
- Cross-domain training on human videos + robot data: humans are trained
  under a JEPA-style latent alignment objective only; robots combine
  alignment with an action-prediction head. A promising path for scaling
  VLA pretraining on action-free web video.

### DIAL: Decoupling Intent and Action via Latent World Modeling
- arXiv: [2603.29844](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.29844)
- Makes the System-2 VLM a **predictive latent world model** and the
  controller a **latent inverse-dynamics model**, enforcing that every
  motor command is grounded in visual foresight through a differentiable
  latent-intent bottleneck. A clean instantiation of dual-system theory
  where the bottleneck is the world-model prediction itself.

### ViVa: Video-Generative Value Model for Robot RL
- arXiv: [2604.08168](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08168v1)
- Repurposes a pretrained video generator as a value model, jointly
  predicting future proprioception and a scalar value. Nice bridge between
  video world models and RL-style value functions for VLAs operating under
  partial observability and delayed feedback.

### Goal-VLA: Image-Generative VLMs as Object-Centric World Models
- arXiv: [2506.23919](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.23919v3)
- Zero-shot manipulation by turning an image-generative VLM into an
  object-centric world model that supplies goal images.

## 5. Latent Action Embedding

### LAWM: Latent Action Pretraining Through World Modeling
- arXiv: [2509.18428](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.18428v1)
- Model-agnostic self-supervised pretraining that learns latent actions
  from unlabeled video (robots *or* humans) through world modeling. A
  strong candidate for "ImageNet moment" of action representations.

### **[Highlight]** WholeBodyVLA: Unified Latent VLA for Whole-Body Loco-Manipulation (ICLR 2026)
- Code: [github.com/OpenDriveLab/WholebodyVLA](https://github.com/OpenDriveLab/WholebodyVLA)
- Learns unified latent actions from action-free egocentric video via a
  Latent Action Model (LAM), then trains a loco-manipulation-oriented
  RL policy on top. One of the cleaner treatments of the whole-body
  coordination problem for humanoids.

## 6. RL for VLA (Post-Training & Infrastructure)

### **[Highlight]** RLinf-VLA: Unified and Efficient RL Framework for VLA
- arXiv: [2510.06710](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.06710) · Code:
  [github.com/RLinf/RLinf](https://github.com/RLinf/RLinf)
- General-purpose RL post-training infrastructure for VLAs. Reports
  **98.11%** on 130 LIBERO tasks, **97.66%** on 25 ManiSkill tasks, and
  84.63% average on 6 RoboTwin tasks. Given how much VLA work is
  bottlenecked by RL tooling, a good open framework is disproportionately
  valuable.

### VLA-RL: Scalable RL for Masterful Manipulation
- arXiv: [2505.18719](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18719)
- One of the early attempts at making VLA RL post-training scale in the
  same way SFT has.

### SimpleVLA-RL
- arXiv: [2509.09674](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.09674v1)
- VLA-specialized trajectory sampling + multi-env rendering on top of veRL.

### TwinRL-VLA: Digital-Twin-Driven RL
- arXiv: [2602.09023](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.09023)
- Couples a digital twin with real-world rollouts to scale exploration.

### RobustVLA: Robustness-Aware RL Post-Training
- arXiv: [2511.01331](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.01331)
- Specifically targets OOD robustness during post-training.

## 7. Benchmarks & Simulators

### RoboPlayground: Democratizing Robotic Evaluation
- arXiv: [2604.05226](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.05226v1)
- Users author executable manipulation tasks in natural language within a
  structured physical domain. Helps with the "every lab ships its own
  simulator" problem.

### UAV-Track VLA + Benchmark
- arXiv: [2604.02241](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02241)
- 890K-frame, 176-task, 85-object multimodal tracking benchmark in CARLA,
  plus a π0.5-based VLA with a temporal-compression net for aerial
  tracking.

### VLABench / StaticEmbodiedBench / RoboChallenge
- [VLABench](https://vlabench.github.io/) — 100 task categories, 2000+
  objects, language-conditioned manipulation.
- [StaticEmbodiedBench](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.06553) — plug-and-play
  static-scene evaluation across 42 scenarios / 8 dimensions.
- [RoboChallenge](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.17950) — large-scale
  real-robot evaluation of embodied policies.

## 8. Applications (Driving / Aerial / Autonomous Agents)

### UniDriveVLA: Unifying Understanding, Perception, Planning
- arXiv: [2604.02190](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02190)
- Mixture-of-Transformers VLA that resolves the perception-vs-reasoning
  conflict for driving via expert decoupling.

### LMGenDrive: LLM Understanding + Generative World Model for Driving
- arXiv: [2604.08719](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.08719v1)
- Couples a multimodal LLM with a generative world model for closed-loop
  end-to-end driving.

### MVAdapt: Zero-Shot Multi-Vehicle Adaptation
- arXiv: [2604.11854](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.11854)
- Physics-conditioned adaptation for transferring end-to-end driving
  across vehicle embodiments. Quantifies the vehicle-domain gap and
  proposes a frozen-scene-encoder + physics-encoder + cross-attention
  recipe.

### VLA-Forget: Unlearning for Embodied Foundation Models
- arXiv: [2604.03956](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.03956v1)
- Formalizes VLA unlearning as targeted forgetting + perceptual
  preservation + reasoning retention, and proposes a hybrid
  ratio-aware + significance-based selective-editing pipeline
  compatible with OpenVLA.

## Curator's Top 3 of the Day

1. **StarVLA** ([2604.05014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05014)) — the
   infrastructure the VLA community has been missing. If it sticks, ablations
   across papers will finally be comparable.
2. **ProGAL-VLA** ([2604.09824](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09824)) —
   rigorously addresses a failure mode (language ignorance) that
   every deployed VLA quietly suffers from.
3. **HY-Embodied-0.5** ([2604.07430](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07430)) —
   clearest articulation to date of an *embodied* foundation model stack,
   with a credible edge-deployment story via on-policy distillation.

Runner-ups: **SnapFlow** (the efficiency win of the week), **MemoryVLA**
(the memory template to beat), **VLA-JEPA** (the pretraining
recipe to watch).
